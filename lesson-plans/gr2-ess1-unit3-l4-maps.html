Maps and Directions

Earth Science Unit 3, Lesson 4

📚 Grade: 2nd Grade ⏱️ Duration: 50 minutes 🔬 Subject: Earth Science: Maps 🧰 Kit Lesson: No
🗺️🧭📐✏️
Maps help us understand Earth's surface! Scientists and explorers use maps to find their way.
📋 NGSS Standard:
2-ESS2-2: Map the shapes and kinds of land and bodies of water in an area and where water on Earth is found.

🎯 Learning Goals

  • Identify the key elements of a map: title, key/legend, compass rose, and symbols.
  • Draw a map of a familiar space (room or yard) using map conventions.
  • Use cardinal directions (North, South, East, West) to describe locations.

💡 Background

Maps are tools that show Earth's surface (or part of it) from above. A good map has several key elements: a title (what the map shows), a key or legend (what each symbol means), a compass rose (showing North, South, East, West directions), and a scale (showing how distances on the map compare to real distances). Scientists use maps to track landforms, water, weather, and much more. Even ancient peoples drew maps to navigate and share information about the land.

💡 Key vocabulary: map, symbol, key, compass rose, scale

📦 Materials

🏠 What You Need

  • Paper (at least 2 sheets per student)
  • Pencil
  • Crayons or markers
  • Ruler (optional)
  • A simple map to examine (a neighborhood map, park map, or map in a book)

✅ All household items, nothing to buy!

📖 Vocabulary

mapa drawing that shows an area from above, using symbols to represent real features
symbola small drawing or shape on a map that represents something real
keya list that explains what each symbol on a map means (also called a legend)
compass rosea symbol on a map showing the four main directions: North, South, East, West
scalea guide showing how map distances relate to real distances

🔬 Procedure

Step 1 (10 min): Examine a Real Map: Look at a simple map together. Identify: what is the title? Where is the compass rose? What symbols are used? Does it have a scale?
Step 2 (5 min): Plan Your Map: Look around the room (or plan to map the yard). What features will you include? Furniture, doors, windows, plants, trees?
Step 3 (20 min): Draw the Map: Draw the room or yard from above (bird's eye view). Include: title, at least 5 symbols with a key, and a compass rose. Add color to make it clear.
Step 4 (10 min): Share and Compare: If both parent and student draw the same space, compare the maps. What is similar? What is different? Which details did each person notice?
Step 5 (5 min): Discussion and Wrap-Up: Ask the discussion questions and complete the worksheet.

💬 Discussion Questions

🗣️ Q1: If your map has a compass rose showing North at the top, which direction is the front door from the living room?

Suggested answer: This depends on their specific map! The learning here is that students can use their compass rose to describe directions. Practice: 'The window is to the North of the sofa.' Or 'The door is to the East.' If the compass rose is correct, these should match reality.

🗣️ Q2: Why do all maps need a key (legend)? What would happen without one?

Suggested answer: Without a key, map readers would not know what each symbol represents. A symbol might mean a mountain or a building, and you would not know which. The key is like a translation guide for the map's special language of symbols.

🚀 Extensions

  • 🌍 Treasure Map: Draw a map of the house with a hidden 'treasure' (a toy or treat). Give the map to a family member and have them find it using the directions!
  • 📱 Compare to Google Maps: Look at your house on Google Maps (satellite view). Compare to your hand-drawn map. What is the same? What did you miss?
  • 📚 Book Connection: 'Me on the Map' by Joan Swanson

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Bird's eye view challenge: Some students find top-down perspective difficult at first. Practice by looking straight down at a pencil or book on the table and drawing what you see from above.
  • Cardinal directions tip: Use 'Never Eat Soggy Waffles' as a memory trick for North, East, South, West (going clockwise).
  • NGSS link: 2-ESS2-2 asks students to map shapes and kinds of land. Drawing a map with a legend and compass rose directly fulfills this standard.
