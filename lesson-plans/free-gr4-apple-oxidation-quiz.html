✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Prompt: Use your data to explain which treatment slowed browning best and why the control mattered.

  • Use the quiz and short response together: The multiple-choice section checks recognition, while the short response checks whether the student can explain an idea with evidence.
  • Good evidence sounds specific: Look for references to the actual materials, observations, comparison tests, or redesign notes from the lesson.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

Detach note: This page is for parent/teacher reference. Student wording may vary if the science idea is correct.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. 1. Oxidation
  2. 2. To show what happens without the treatment
  3. 3. The liquid treatment applied to each slice
  4. 4. A careful prediction you can test
  5. 5. Cut similar slices and check them at the same times
  6. 6. That treatment slowed oxidation more than the others tested
  7. 7. Observations about color change over time
  8. 8. So you know which treatment produced each result

What Success Looks Like

  • Apple browning is oxidation
  • A control shows what happens without treatment
  • The liquid treatment is the variable

Short Response Guidance

  • Student states a correct science idea from the lesson.
  • Student uses one real observation, comparison, or test result as evidence.
  • Student connects the evidence to the idea instead of only restating a vocabulary word.
  • Strong answers should cite data such as “the control browned the most after 30 minutes, but the lemon-juice slice stayed lighter,” not just a guess about which liquid should work.
