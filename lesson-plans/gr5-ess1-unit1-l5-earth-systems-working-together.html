Earth Systems Working Together

Grade 5 Earth & Space Science — Water on Earth Systems | Unit 1, Lesson 5 of 6

📚 Grade: 5th Grade⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes🔬 Subject: Earth & Space Science — Water on Earth Systems
🌦️🌍🌱
Water connects Earth’s air, land, water, and living things.
📋 NGSS Standard:
5-ESS2-1 — Develop a model using an example to describe ways the geosphere, biosphere, hydrosphere, and/or atmosphere interact.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will identify how Earth’s spheres interact through water movement.

💡 The Big Idea

Water links Earth’s systems: the atmosphere, hydrosphere, geosphere, and biosphere.

🔬 The Science

Science Snapshot
Atmosphere, geosphere, hydrosphere, and biosphere constantly exchange water and influence one another.

What is happening?

Earth’s water does not move through the atmosphere, land, oceans, and living things separately. Those systems interact all the time, so a change in one part can affect the others.

Why it works that way

For example, mountains shape rainfall patterns, plants slow runoff and return water vapor to the air, and oceans store heat that affects weather. Scientists study these linked systems because Earth works through many connected causes, not isolated events.

Look for this in the lesson

Students should practice seeing one event from several angles: where the water is, what land it moves over, what the weather is doing, and how living things change the pathway. That systems thinking is the heart of the lesson.

Ask while you read: If one Earth system changes, which other systems might respond?

📦 Materials

🏠 Household Items to Gather

  • Earth systems cards or chart
  • Worksheet and pencil

📋 Printables

  • Student worksheet page from this lesson

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Name the spheres (5–10 min)
Review what belongs in each Earth system.
Step 2 — Follow one water story (10 min)
Example: rain falls on soil, soaks in, and is taken up by a plant.
Step 3 — Label each interaction (10 min)
Students identify which systems are connected in each step.
Step 4 — Build a systems map (10 min)
Draw a diagram with arrows between the spheres.
Step 5 — Explain why water is the connector (10 min)
Use the map to explain how the spheres depend on one another.

💬 Discussion Questions

What is one example of water connecting the atmosphere and geosphere?Rain falling onto land.
How can water connect the geosphere and biosphere?Plants can take up water from soil.
Why are Earth systems better understood together?The systems affect one another, not just themselves.

🚀 Extensions

Extension: Describe a local example, such as a puddle, river, snowpack, or garden, using all four Earth systems.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: Students may confuse hydrosphere with atmosphere. Keep examples concrete and tied to water.
