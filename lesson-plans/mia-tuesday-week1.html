📊 Family Survey 15 min

Data & Statistics — Collecting and Displaying Data

You're a real data scientist today! 🧪 Your mission: come up with a fun question, survey your family, and turn the results into a chart.

  1. Pick a question (or make up your own!) from the ideas below.
  2. Survey at least 4 people — family members, pets count if you answer for them! 🐶
  3. Record the data in the tally table on the worksheet.
  4. Make a bar chart OR pie chart using the blank templates.

🎯 Survey Question Ideas:

🍕 What's the best pizza topping?
🌈 What's your favorite season?
🎬 What movie genre is the best?
🐾 If you could be any animal, which one?
💡 Data Scientist Tip: Make sure your question has 3–6 answer choices — too many makes the chart hard to read, too few makes it boring!

🔍 Website Scan 10 min

Digital Literacy — Navigating Informational Websites

Time to be a web detective! 🌐 Visit one of the kid-friendly websites below and find 3 specific facts using the site's headings, search bar, and navigation tools.

  1. Pick a website from the suggestions (or use one your parent approves).
  2. Choose a topic you're curious about — animals, space, history, anything!
  3. Use headings, menus, and the search bar to navigate (don't just scroll randomly!).
  4. Record 3 facts you found and how you found them on the worksheet.

🧠 Prediction + Proof 10 min

Reading — Predicting & Using Text Evidence

Stop reading your book mid-chapter! ✋ Before you turn the next page, predict what's going to happen — and explain why you think so. Then keep reading and see if you were right!

  1. Read until you're halfway through a chapter.
  2. STOP and fill in the "Before" section of the worksheet.
  3. Make your prediction — what will happen next?
  4. Explain why using clues from the text.
  5. Finish the chapter, then fill in the "After" section.
✅ Example — A Strong Prediction

What's happening right now: Percy just found out Luke stole the lightning bolt. Luke is blocking the exit and has a sword.

My prediction: I think Percy is going to fight Luke but won't be able to beat him alone — someone will come help.

Why I think this: The author keeps showing that Percy wins by working with his friends (like with Annabeth and Grover on the quest). Also, Luke is described as the best swordsman at camp, so Percy probably can't beat him 1-on-1 yet.

👆 Notice: uses text clues + patterns from the story — not just a random guess!

💡 Prediction Power: The best predictions use clues the author already gave you — character behavior, foreshadowing, and patterns. It's like being a detective of the story!

✏️ Plan Before You Write 10 min

Writing — Planning & Organization

Great writers don't just start writing — they plan first! 🗺️ Today you'll spend 5 minutes organizing your ideas, then 5 minutes writing from your plan.

  1. Pick a writing prompt from the list below.
  2. Spend 5 minutes filling out the graphic organizer on the worksheet.
  3. Spend 5 minutes writing your piece using your plan — on the lined page.

✨ Writing Prompts (pick one!):

  • Describe your dream treehouse — what's in it, where is it, and why is it the best?
  • You just discovered a hidden room in your school. What's inside?
  • Write about a time you tried something hard and surprised yourself.
  • Convince someone to visit your favorite place on Earth. Why is it so special?
  • You get to create a new holiday. What is it, and how do people celebrate it?
💡 Planning Power: A 5-minute plan can make your writing twice as good. When you know where you're going, the words flow way easier!
