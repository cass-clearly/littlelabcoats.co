## Rachel round-1 review notes

1. **Target:** `gr3-ess2-unit1-lesson1-weather-patterns-over-time.html`  
   **Observation:** The first lesson needed to feel more concretely about multi-day observation instead of reading like a generic weather scaffold.  
   **Expected revision action:** Add a day-by-day weather record workflow, a pattern table, and evidence-specific answer-key language.

2. **Target:** `gr3-ess2-unit1-lesson2-climate-regions-and-conditions.html`  
   **Observation:** Climate needed a stronger contrast with weather so students would not leave the lesson still mixing up “today” with “usual over time.”  
   **Expected revision action:** Tighten the science explanation, worksheet sort, and sentence-level contrast language.

3. **Target:** `gr3-ess2-unit1-lesson3-reading-weather-data.html`  
   **Observation:** The data-reading lesson needed clearer chart/graph interpretation tasks rather than a generic evidence lesson shell.  
   **Expected revision action:** Add chart-reading prompts and claim-with-evidence work tied to a display.

4. **Target:** `gr3-ess2-unit1-lesson4-weather-hazards-and-impacts.html`  
   **Observation:** Hazard language was on track, but students needed stronger cause/effect support connecting each event to its likely impact.  
   **Expected revision action:** Add hazard-to-impact worksheet rows and clearer impact examples in the answer key.

5. **Target:** `gr3-ess2-unit1-lesson5-designing-for-local-weather.html` + `gr3-ess2-unit1-lesson6-reducing-weather-related-hazards.html`  
   **Observation:** The final lessons needed a clearer engineering-through-Earth-Science bridge.  
   **Expected revision action:** Make the design comparison and merit claim language explicitly about local weather hazards.

6. **Target:** `gr3-ess2-unit1-refcard-lesson1-weather-patterns-over-time.html` through `gr3-ess2-unit1-refcard-lesson6-reducing-weather-related-hazards.html`  
   **Observation:** The refcards were structurally aligned but some wording still felt placeholder-ish.  
   **Expected revision action:** Replace placeholder vocabulary/support wording with lesson-specific Earth Science copy.

7. **Target:** `gr3-ess2-unit1-quiz.html`  
   **Observation:** The quiz shell was strong, but the short response needed to require hazard evidence plus a reason a solution has merit.  
   **Expected revision action:** Tighten the prompt, grading cues, and answer-key success criteria around evidence-backed merit claims.

## Margaret round-1 review notes

1. **Target:** `3-ESS2 Unit 1 lesson set`  
   **Observation:** The unit direction was right, but the homeschool coaching layer needed to be stronger than the earlier draft scaffolds.  
   **Expected revision action:** Strengthen student-facing and parent-facing scaffolds without changing the shell.

2. **Target:** `lesson1` + `lesson2`  
   **Observation:** Weather vs climate needed simpler language and clearer examples because these lessons set the unit frame.  
   **Expected revision action:** Add more concrete examples and stronger contrast wording.

3. **Target:** `lesson3` + `lesson4`  
   **Observation:** Data and hazard lessons needed more specific evidence use, not broad explanation only.  
   **Expected revision action:** Make prompts more anchored to chart details, observations, and impacts.

4. **Target:** `lesson5` + `lesson6`  
   **Observation:** The design-solution lessons needed more direct “this solution has merit because…” coaching.  
   **Expected revision action:** Add sentence frames and stronger merit/evidence support in worksheets and answer keys.

5. **Target:** `refcards + quiz`  
   **Observation:** Support assets were close, but they needed final Earth-Science-specific wording to feel finished.  
   **Expected revision action:** Replace generic support phrasing with final topic-specific copy and preserve the compact support format.
