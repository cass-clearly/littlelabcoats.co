Landforms All Around

Earth Science Unit 3, Lesson 1

📚 Grade: 2nd Grade ⏱️ Duration: 50 minutes 🔬 Subject: Earth Science: Earth's Features 🧰 Kit Lesson: No
⛰️🏔️🏝️🌊
Earth's surface has mountains, valleys, plains, and islands! Let's explore landforms.
📋 NGSS Standard:
2-ESS2-2: Map the shapes and kinds of land and bodies of water in an area and where water on Earth is found.

🎯 Learning Goals

  • Name and describe six major types of landforms.
  • Build model landforms from playdough.
  • Identify landforms on a simple map.

💡 Background

Earth's surface is not flat! It has many different shapes called landforms. Mountains rise high above the surrounding land. Valleys are low areas between mountains. Plains are flat, wide areas. Hills are smaller than mountains. A peninsula is land mostly surrounded by water on three sides. An island is land completely surrounded by water. These landforms were formed by volcanoes, moving tectonic plates, water erosion, and ice over millions of years.

💡 Key vocabulary: landform, mountain, valley, plain, peninsula, island

📦 Materials

🏠 What You Need

  • Playdough (homemade: mix 2 cups flour, 1 cup salt, 1 cup water, 1 tsp oil) OR store bought
  • A large piece of paper or cardboard for the landscape
  • Pencil and crayons for labeling
  • Blue marker or crayon to draw water features

✅ All household items, nothing to buy!

📖 Vocabulary

landforma natural feature of Earth's surface, such as a mountain or valley
mountaina very high landform with a peak
valleya low area of land between mountains or hills, often with a river
plaina large, flat area of land
peninsulaland mostly surrounded by water but connected to a larger landmass
islandland completely surrounded by water

🔬 Procedure

Step 1 (10 min): Introduce Landforms: Read each definition aloud. Use hand gestures: raise hands high for mountain, hold hands flat for plain, bring hands down for valley, spread fingers for delta.
Step 2 (5 min): Look at a Map: Find a simple map image in a book or atlas (or draw one). Identify mountains, valleys, plains, coastlines, islands.
Step 3 (20 min): Build Landforms with Playdough: Build at least 3 landforms on cardboard. Make a mountain, a valley, and an island surrounded by blue crayon water. Label each one.
Step 4 (10 min): Map the Playdough Landscape: On paper, draw a top-down map of the playdough landscape. Include a key showing what each shape represents.
Step 5 (5 min): Discussion and Wrap-Up: Ask the discussion questions and complete the worksheet.

💬 Discussion Questions

🗣️ Q1: Florida is surrounded by water on three sides. What kind of landform is it?

Suggested answer: Florida is a peninsula! It is connected to the rest of the United States at the top (north), but surrounded by water on the east, south, and west sides. Hawaii is different: it is an island because it is completely surrounded by water.

🗣️ Q2: How do you think mountains were formed? What about valleys?

Suggested answer: Mountains form when tectonic plates of rock push against each other, pushing rock upward (like crumpling a tablecloth). Valleys often form when rivers carve through rock over millions of years, or when glaciers (huge sheets of ice) slowly scrape through the land.

🚀 Extensions

  • 🌋 Volcano Model: Add a vinegar-and-baking-soda volcano to your playdough mountain to see how volcanic eruptions build landforms.
  • 🗺️ Find It on a Map: Look at a US or world map and find one example of each landform you learned about.
  • 📚 Book Connection: 'National Geographic Kids: Everything Rocks and Minerals' or 'Earthshake' by Lisa Westberg Peters

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Playdough recipe: Mix 2 cups flour, 1 cup salt, 1 cup water, 1 tsp oil. Knead until smooth. Add food coloring if desired. Stores in an airtight bag for several days.
  • Landform review: Mountain (high, peaked), Hill (smaller than mountain), Valley (low, between hills), Plain (flat, wide), Peninsula (3 sides water), Island (all sides water).
  • NGSS link: 2-ESS2-2 asks students to map shapes and kinds of land. The map exercise directly fulfills this standard.
