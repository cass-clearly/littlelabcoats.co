Bread Mold Terrarium Reference Card

Grade 5
Mold is a decomposer fungus that grows best under certain conditions and returns nutrients to ecosystems.
Key vocabulary
fungusdecomposersporesvariable
What to notice
  • Mold is alive even when you cannot see it at first.
  • Moisture strongly affects mold growth.
  • A sealed setup keeps the experiment safer.
  • Comparing conditions helps identify what mold needs most.
Materials snapshot
  • Bread slices
  • Sealable bags or containers
  • Water spray
  • Labels
  • Observation chart
Talk-about prompts
  • Which condition produced the most mold growth?
  • Why are spores important?
  • Why should the bags stay sealed?
  • How does decomposition help ecosystems?
Why it matters

Mold is not just gross — it is an ecosystem cleanup crew. As a decomposer, it breaks down dead material and helps return nutrients to the environment so other living things can keep growing.

Try this next: Set up two or three sealed bags with different moisture or light conditions and compare growth over several days.
