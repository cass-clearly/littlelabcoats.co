Explain How Energy Moves and Causes Motion

Physical Science — Energy & Motion | Unit 1, Lesson 6 of 6

📚 Grade: 4th Grade⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes🔬 Subject: Physical Science — Energy & Motion
🧠📝
A strong Grade 4 explanation links a claim to evidence from collisions, speed tests, and friction investigations.
📋 NGSS Standard:
4-PS3-1 — Use evidence to construct an explanation relating the speed of an object to the energy of that object. / 4-PS3-3 — Ask questions and predict outcomes about the changes in energy that occur when objects collide.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will construct an evidence-based explanation describing how energy moved and caused motion changes in the unit investigations.

💡 The Big Idea

Science explanations are strongest when students connect a clear claim to specific evidence and explain why the evidence fits the claim.

🔬 The Science

Throughout this unit, students observed objects start moving after collisions, change more with greater speed, and slow sooner on rougher surfaces. These patterns support the idea that energy can move and motion can change for observable reasons.

This final lesson asks students to pull together evidence from multiple investigations. They should name the event, describe what changed, and explain why that evidence supports the claim.

Grade 4 students are ready to distinguish between a claim, the evidence, and the reasoning that links them.

🧪 Synthesis

Step 1 — Review unit evidence (10 min)
List what students observed in collision, speed, and surface tests.
Step 2 — Choose one claim (10 min)
Select a claim such as “Energy moved during a collision” or “Friction changed motion.”
Step 3 — Gather evidence (10 min)
Students pull at least two specific observations or measurements from the unit.
Step 4 — Add reasoning (10 min)
Explain how the evidence supports the claim about energy and motion.
Step 5 — Share and revise (10 min)
Read the explanation aloud and improve any weak parts.

💬 Discussion Questions

What makes evidence stronger than a guess?Evidence comes from observations, measurements, or recorded results.
Why is one example sometimes weaker than two or three?Multiple examples show a pattern, not just a single event.
What should the reasoning part do?It should explain why the evidence supports the claim.

🚀 Extensions

Extension: Turn the final explanation into a mini poster with one claim, two evidence boxes, and one reasoning box.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: Model one short claim-evidence-reasoning example before students write independently.

Misconception to catch: Vocabulary alone is not an explanation. Students still need a real claim and supporting evidence.

🔑 PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Sample explanation

In the collision lesson, the second car started moving after the first car hit it. In the speed lesson, the faster start caused a bigger change. In the surface lesson, the rough surface slowed motion sooner. Together, that evidence shows energy can move and cause motion changes for reasons we can observe.

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Student makes a clear claim.
  • Student includes at least two specific pieces of evidence.
  • Student explains why the evidence supports the claim about energy and motion.
