🐙 The Amazing Octopus

Octopuses are some of the most incredible creatures in the ocean. They have three hearts — two pump blood to their gills, and one pumps it to the rest of the body. When an octopus swims, the heart that delivers blood to the body actually stops beating, which is why they prefer crawling to swimming!

These animals are also masters of disguise. An octopus can change its color, texture, and even shape in less than a second. Scientists have watched octopuses mimic the appearance of rocks, coral, and even other animals like lionfish and sea snakes to avoid predators.

Perhaps most surprising is how smart they are. Octopuses have been observed opening jars from the inside, solving mazes, and even using coconut shells as portable shelters — which scientists consider a form of tool use. Each of their eight arms contains clusters of neurons, meaning their arms can taste, touch, and even "think" independently!

Sadly, octopuses have very short lifespans — most live only 1 to 2 years. But in that short time, they pack in a whole lot of brilliance.

🧠 Theme Detective 10 min

Reading — Theme & Evidence

Every story has a hidden message — a theme. Your job as a Theme Detective is to read the fable below, figure out the theme, and find 3 pieces of evidence that prove it! 🔎

  1. Read the fable carefully.
  2. Think: What lesson is this story trying to teach?
  3. Find 3 moments in the story that support the theme.
  4. Fill out the detective worksheet!

🦅 The Crow and the Pitcher

On a scorching summer day, a crow flew for miles searching for water. Her throat was dry and her wings ached. At last she spotted a tall pitcher sitting in a garden. She swooped down eagerly and peered inside.

There was water at the bottom — but the pitcher was too tall and narrow for her beak to reach it. She tried tipping it over, but it was too heavy. She tried breaking it with her beak, but the clay was too thick.

The crow sat on the rim, exhausted. She was about to give up and fly away when she noticed a pile of small pebbles nearby. An idea sparked in her mind.

One by one, she picked up pebbles and dropped them into the pitcher. Plink. Plink. Plink. Slowly, the water began to rise. After many trips back and forth, the water finally reached the top.

The crow drank deeply, her thirst finally quenched. She ruffled her feathers proudly and flew off into the sky, stronger than before.

— Adapted from Aesop's Fables

🔑 What is a Theme?

A theme is the life lesson or message the author wants you to learn. It's NOT a one-word topic (like "water" or "birds"). It's a full sentence, like: "Hard work pays off" or "True friends stick together in tough times."

✏️ Sentence Combining 10 min

Writing — Sentence Structure

Short, choppy sentences are boring! 😴 Your mission: take the choppy sentences and combine them into 1–2 smooth, well-structured sentences. Use words like and, but, because, although, while, who, which, when to connect ideas.

✅ Example

Choppy: The dog was big. The dog was brown. The dog liked to swim.

Combined: The big, brown dog loved to swim.
