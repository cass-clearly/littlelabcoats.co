✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Explain one trait that offspring can inherit from parents and describe evidence from the unit that shows variation in that trait.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Use the quiz and short response together: recognition plus explanation gives a stronger picture of understanding.
  • Good evidence sounds specific: look for vocabulary, observations, and a clear reasoned explanation.
  • Fast grading tip: scan for whether the student uses unit vocabulary correctly and connects evidence to their claim.

✂️ PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Parent note: Student wording may vary if the science reasoning is correct.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. Students will identify observable traits and explain that many traits are inherited from parents.
  2. Students will compare offspring plants with parent plants and describe inherited plant traits.
  3. Students will analyze simple animal data to identify inherited animal traits.
  4. Students will identify variation in traits within a group of similar organisms.
  5. Students will interpret a simple class data set to support claims about inherited traits and variation.
  6. Students will analyze and interpret trait evidence to explain inheritance and variation in a final model or argument.
  7. Using evidence from observations, tests, or models to support explanations
  8. To make relationships easier to see and explain

Short Response — What Success Looks Like

  • Student names a real inherited trait.
  • Student explains that offspring are similar to parents but not identical.
  • Student uses evidence from an observation, model, or class data table.

Short Response — Sample Acceptable Answer

A kitten might inherit fur color from its parents, but the kittens in one litter can still have variation. Our class data showed that similar organisms can share traits and still look a little different.
