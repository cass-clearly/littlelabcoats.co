Leaves and Making Food

Grade 4 · Unit 2
Leaves are not just decorations. Their structure helps plants capture sunlight and exchange gases so the plant can make food.
Key vocabulary
leafsunlightphotosynthesischlorophyll
What to notice
  • Compare a few leaves and ask what features many leaves have in common.
  • Guide students to notice broad flat surfaces and why those surfaces might help with gathering light.
  • Explain at a Grade 4 level that leaves help plants make food from light, water, and air.
Materials snapshot
  • printed lesson pages and Student Worksheet
  • several leaves or leaf photos for comparison
  • flashlight or sunny window if available
  • pencil
Talk-about prompts
  • Why might a broad flat leaf be helpful?
  • Why do plants need a structure that helps make food?
  • How can the structure of a leaf give evidence about its function?
Why it matters

Leaves often have a broad, thin surface that helps them capture sunlight. The green color comes from chlorophyll, which helps in photosynthesis. Plants use light energy, water, and carbon dioxide to make food. Leaf structure matters because the plant needs a surface that can gather light effectively while supporting the plant’s survival and growth.

Try this next: Compare a thick needle-like leaf and a broad flat leaf. Ask which seems best for collecting lots of light and what tradeoffs the plant might face in different environments.
