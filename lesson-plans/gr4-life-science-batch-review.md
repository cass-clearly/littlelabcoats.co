## Full-domain 4-LS1-1 coverage check

### Performance expectation
`4-LS1-1` — Construct an argument that plants and animals have internal and external structures that function to support survival, growth, behavior, and reproduction.

### Unit 1 coverage — animal structures
- Lesson 1: visible external structures linked to survival
- Lesson 2: sensing structures linked to behavior and survival
- Lesson 3: feeding structures linked to survival and growth
- Lesson 4: movement structures linked to behavior and safety
- Lesson 5: protection/defense structures linked to survival
- Lesson 6: argument construction using multiple animal examples

### Unit 2 coverage — plant structures
- Lesson 1: roots and stems linked to survival and growth
- Lesson 2: leaves linked to food-making and growth
- Lesson 3: internal transport structures linked to survival/growth
- Lesson 4: flowers linked to reproduction
- Lesson 5: seeds and fruits linked to reproduction and offspring success
- Lesson 6: argument construction using multiple plant examples

### Domain-level coverage summary
- **Internal structures:** plant water transport pathways; animal sensing/internal structure framing retained through 4-LS1-1 language
- **External structures:** animal body parts; plant roots, stems, leaves, flowers, fruits, seeds
- **Survival:** Units 1 and 2 both address structures that help organisms stay alive
- **Growth:** plant support/food/water transport plus animal feeding structures
- **Behavior:** explicitly covered in Unit 1 through sensing and movement structures
- **Reproduction:** explicitly covered in Unit 2 through flowers, pollination, seeds, and fruits
- **Construct an argument:** addressed directly in both Unit 1 Lesson 6 and Unit 2 Lesson 6

Disposition: the 12-lesson Grade 4 Life Science domain now covers the expected strands of `4-LS1-1` at the package level.
