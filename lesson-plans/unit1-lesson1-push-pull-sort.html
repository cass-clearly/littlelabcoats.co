👐 The Great Push & Pull Sort 👐

Physical Science — Unit 1, Lesson 1

📚 Grade: Kindergarten ⏱️ Duration: 30–45 minutes 🔬 Subject: Physical Science — Forces
🏗️ ← → 🚂
Everything that moves gets a push or a pull — can you figure out which is which?
📋 NGSS Standard:
K-PS2-1 — Plan and conduct an investigation to compare the effects of different strengths or different directions of pushes and pulls on the motion of an object
K-PS2-2 — Analyze data to determine if a design solution works as intended

🎯 The Big Idea

Things move because something pushes them or pulls them! A push moves something away from you. A pull moves something toward you. In this lesson, your kids will explore real objects and everyday actions, then sort them into push and pull categories.

🔬 The Science

A force is a push or a pull that makes things move, stop, or change direction. When you push something, you use force to move it away from you. When you pull something, you use force to bring it closer. Forces are invisible, but we can see what they do — they make things move! Everything that moves had a push or a pull to start it going.

💡 Key vocabulary: push, pull, force, motion, direction, sort

📦 Materials

🏠 Household Items to Gather

  • A toy car or ball (to push across the floor)
  • A wagon, sled, or laundry basket with a rope/handle (to pull)
  • A drawer (to push shut and pull open)
  • A door (push & pull demonstration)
  • A zipper jacket or backpack
  • Rubber band or hair tie (to stretch/pull)
  • A chair (to push in, pull out from table)

🧰 For the Sorting Activity

  • Printed sorting mat (last pages of this lesson)
  • Printed picture cards — cut apart (included)
  • Scissors ✂️
  • Glue stick (if you want cards glued permanently)
  • Crayons or markers (for coloring & bonus drawing)

🔬 Let's Get Started

Step 1 — Warm-Up: Push Me, Pull Me (5 min)
Stand up with the kids. Say: "I'm going to do something, and you tell me — is it a push or a pull?"
  • Push a toy car across the floor → "PUSH!"
  • Pull open a drawer → "PULL!"
  • Push a door closed → "PUSH!"
  • Pull a wagon toward you → "PULL!"
Let the kids take turns doing each one. Have them feel the difference in their hands.
Step 2 — Body Moves (5 min)
"Can your body push and pull?"
  • Push your hands against each other → PUSH
  • Pull a rubber band / stretch it → PULL
  • Push a pillow off the couch → PUSH
  • Pull your sibling gently by the hand → PULL
Key question: "When you push, which way does the thing move? When you pull?"
💡 Sibling tip: Have one child push a ball to the other, and the other push it back. Then try pulling a blanket together from opposite ends — tug-of-war style. They'll feel the force.
Step 3 — Introduce the Sort (3 min)
Give each child a sorting mat (printed from page 2). Show them the picture cards. Say: "Each card shows something being pushed or pulled. Your job is to figure out which it is and put it on the right side of your mat!"
Step 4 — The Great Sort! (10–15 min)
  • Cut out the picture cards together (great for fine motor skills!)
  • One card at a time — kids discuss with each other: "Is this a push or a pull?"
  • Place (or glue) each card on the correct side of the sorting mat
  • If they disagree, that's great! Have them explain their thinking
Step 5 — Check & Discuss (5–10 min)
Go through each card together. Use the discussion questions below. Celebrate what they got right and gently talk through any tricky ones.
Step 6 — Bonus Challenge: Draw Your Own! (5 min)
On the back of the sorting mat (or a blank paper), each child draws:
🔴 One thing they can PUSH    🔵 One thing they can PULL
Label them and share with each other!

💬 Discussion Questions

🗣️ Talk about these together:
  • What does your body do when it pushes something? What about when it pulls?
  • Can the same thing be pushed and pulled? (Think about a door, a drawer, a wagon…)
  • What happens if you push something really hard vs. really softly?
  • Can you push or pull something without touching it? (Hint: think about magnets!)
  • What would happen if nobody ever pushed or pulled anything? Would things move on their own?
  • Which is easier for you — pushing or pulling? Why do you think so?

🚀 Extensions

  • 🧲 Magnet Mystery: Get a magnet and some paper clips. Can you push and pull without touching? That's a force too.
  • 📐 Ramp Races: Build a ramp from a book or board. Push a car gently, then hard. How does the push strength change how far it goes?
  • 📚 Read Aloud: "Forces Make Things Move" by Kimberly Brubaker Bradley or "Push and Pull" by Patricia Murphy
  • 🎵 Push & Pull Song: Make up a song together! "Push it away, pull it close — push and pull, that's how it goes!"
  • 🏠 House Hunt: Walk through the house and find 5 things you push and 5 things you pull. Make a list!

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Keep it physical: Kindergartners learn by doing. The more they push and pull real objects, the better the concept sticks. The sorting mat is reinforcement, not the main event.
  • Tricky cards: Some items can be both push and pull (like a door or a wagon). If kids notice this, that's fantastic thinking. Praise it and discuss.
  • Don't rush the discussion: The conversation is where the learning happens. Let siblings debate — that's science talk.
  • For the younger sibling: If one child is pre-K, let them participate physically (pushing/pulling objects) and place cards with help. They don't need to read the labels.
  • Assessment: Can each child correctly sort at least eight out of 12 cards? Can they explain in their own words what push and pull mean? That's success.

  • 🚪 Pulling a door open

  • 🧦 Pulling on your socks

💡 Notes for Tricky Cards

  • ⚽ Kicking a ball: A kick is a push with your foot! Your foot pushes the ball away.
  • 🏐 Throwing a ball: A throw is a push with your hand — you push it away from your body.
  • 🎉 Pushing a swing: You push the swing away from you. (Note: if a child says "pull" because the swing comes back — great thinking! But the person's action is a push.)
  • 🚪 Door: If a child says a door can be pushed and pulled — they're right. Praise this. For the card, we mean pulling it open toward you.
  • 🧲 Magnet: Great intro to forces that work without touching! A magnet pulls metal toward it.

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Child can sort at least eight of 12 cards correctly.
  • Child can explain: "A push moves things away, a pull moves things toward you."
  • Child can demonstrate a push and a pull with their body.
