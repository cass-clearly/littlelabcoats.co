✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Explain one trait variation that can help an organism survive or reproduce, and support your explanation with evidence from the unit.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Use the quiz and short response together: recognition plus explanation gives a stronger picture of understanding.
  • Good evidence sounds specific: look for vocabulary, observations, and a clear reasoned explanation.
  • Fast grading tip: scan for whether the student uses unit vocabulary correctly and connects evidence to their claim.

✂️ PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Parent note: Student wording may vary if the science reasoning is correct.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. Students will explain that variation in traits can give some individuals survival advantages.
  2. Students will use evidence to explain how camouflage trait differences can help organisms avoid predators or capture prey.
  3. Students will compare feeding traits and explain how certain variations may help individuals get food in different settings.
  4. Students will explain how trait differences in movement can help organisms in different environments.
  5. Students will explain how some trait variations can help organisms attract mates, protect offspring, or reproduce successfully.
  6. Students will use evidence from multiple unit investigations to explain how trait variation may provide advantages in surviving, finding mates, and reproducing.
  7. Using evidence from observations, tests, or models to support explanations
  8. To make relationships easier to see and explain

Short Response — What Success Looks Like

  • Student identifies a variation in a trait.
  • Student connects the variation to survival or reproduction.
  • Student uses a specific example from the unit.

Short Response — Sample Acceptable Answer

A bird with a beak shape that matches its food source can get food more easily. Our comparisons showed that different beak traits can give advantages in different environments.
