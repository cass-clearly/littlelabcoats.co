✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Choose one sky or space pattern from this unit and explain it using evidence and a model. Name one model limitation too.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Use the quiz and short response together: recognition plus explanation gives a stronger picture of understanding.
  • Look for model-based reasoning: strong answers say what the model shows and what evidence supports the claim.
  • Fast grading tip: scan for claim, evidence, and reasoning, not vocabulary alone.

✂️ PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Parent note: Student wording may vary if the science reasoning is correct.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. Changing shadow length and direction can provide safe evidence.
  2. Earth’s rotation explains day and night.
  3. Moon phases change because we see different amounts of the sunlit half.
  4. Star maps help compare visible star patterns across time or seasons.
  5. A scale model can show relative order and distance patterns.
  6. A final explanation should include claim, evidence, reasoning, and a model.
  7. A model limitation matters because models simplify real systems.
  8. This unit focuses on sky and space patterns rather than duplicating Earth-process landform change.

Short Response — What Success Looks Like

  • Student names a specific investigation or model from the unit.
  • Student cites evidence from an observation, trial, or model.
  • Student explains why the evidence supports the claim.
