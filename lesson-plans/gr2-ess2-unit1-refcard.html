🌦️ Weather Detectives & Wild Storms

Tools · Clouds · Storms · Forecasts · Water · Design
📏
Lesson 1 — Weather Tools
Meteorologists use repeated observations. Rain gauges, wind tools, and sky charts help us collect better evidence than memory alone.
☁️
Lesson 2 — Cloud Clues
Clouds and condensation tell us what water in the air is doing. Puffy, flat, dark, or wispy clouds can give clues about changing weather.
🌪️
Lesson 3 — Severe Weather
Thunderstorms, tornadoes, floods, and blizzards are not the same. Each has different warning signs, risks, and safe responses.
🔮
Lesson 4 — Forecasting
A forecast is an evidence-based prediction. Good forecasts compare multiple clues and patterns instead of making a wild guess.
💧
Lesson 5 — Water in the Air
Water can evaporate into the air, condense into droplets, form clouds, and fall as precipitation. Models help us see that journey.
🏗️
Lesson 6 — Storm Engineering
Engineers use weather science to design better shelters and tools. Testing and redesign improve a storm-safe model.
Remember This
Weather evidence gets stronger when you collect it over time, not just once.
Remember This
Clouds, wind, and water clues help explain what weather may happen next.
Remember This
Science and engineering work together: learn the storm, then design for it.
