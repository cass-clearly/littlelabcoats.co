Unit 1 -- Physical Science, Grade 2

🪨 1 Which of these is a SOLID?
🪨 A wooden block is a solid because it keeps its own shape! You can pick it up and it does not change form. Water and juice are liquids (they flow and take the shape of their container), and air is a gas. Solids keep their shape -- that is the key!
💧 2 A liquid takes the _______ of its container.
💧 Liquids flow and take the shape of whatever container they are in! Pour water into a round bowl -- it forms a round shape. Pour it into a tall glass -- it becomes tall and narrow. That is exactly what makes liquids different from solids (which keep their own shape).
🎈 3 Which state of matter fills ALL the available space in its container?
🎈 Gases spread out to fill every bit of available space! When you blow air into a balloon, the air (a gas) fills the whole inside. If you moved it to a bigger container, the gas would expand to fill that too. Gases have no fixed shape and no fixed size!
🔍 4 What do we call a describing word for how a material looks or feels -- like "rough" or "hard"?
🔍 A property is a describing word for matter! Rough, smooth, hard, soft, heavy, light, flexible, rigid -- all of these are properties. Scientists use properties to describe and compare materials, and engineers use properties to choose the right material for each job.
🧽 5 You can bend a rubber band easily and it snaps back. You cannot bend a rock at all. The rubber band is _______ and the rock is _______.
🧽 The rubber band is flexible (bends easily without breaking) and the rock is rigid (resists bending). Engineers choose flexible or rigid materials depending on the job -- rubber tires need to be flexible to absorb bumps, while building walls need to be rigid to hold weight!
🪣 6 You drop a coin and a wooden pencil into a bowl of water. What happens?
🪣 The coin is made of dense metal -- it sinks. The pencil is made of wood, which has tiny air pockets inside its fibers that make it less dense than water -- it floats! Density (how tightly packed the matter is) determines whether something sinks or floats, not size!
🧂 7 You stir salt into water and it seems to disappear. Where did the salt go?
🧂 When salt dissolves in water, it breaks into particles so tiny we cannot see them -- but the salt is still there! If you taste the water, it is salty. If you evaporate all the water away, the salt crystals come back. Dissolving is not destroying -- the salt is just hiding!
🧊 8 You freeze a cup of water into an ice cube, then let it melt again. Is this change reversible or irreversible?
🧊 Freezing and melting are reversible changes! Water can go back and forth between solid (ice) and liquid many times. The water molecules do not change -- only the state of matter changes. Compare this to toasting bread, which you CANNOT undo. That is the difference between reversible and irreversible!
🍞 9 You toast a piece of bread. It turns brown and crispy. What kind of change is this?
🍞 Toasting bread is an irreversible change! The heat causes a chemical change inside the bread -- new substances are created, the color turns permanently brown, and the texture becomes crispy. No amount of cooling will make it soft and white again. Irreversible means: no going back! Bread is definitely matter (it is a solid), so that last option is always wrong.
🛥️ 10 You build a foil boat but it sinks after only 3 pennies. What should an engineer do NEXT?
🛥️ Engineers always improve their designs! When something does not work, that is data -- it tells you exactly what to fix. Were the walls too short? Was the base too narrow? Did it spring a leak? Analyze what went wrong, make a specific change, and test again. That is the Engineering Design Process: Design, Build, Test, Improve!
