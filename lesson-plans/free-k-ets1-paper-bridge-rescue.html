🌉🧸 Paper Bridge Rescue

Kindergarten Free Unit Sample — K-ETS1 · Engineering Design

📚 Grade: Kindergarten🔬 Domain: Engineering⏱️ Time: 30–45 minutes
Free standalone unit teaser: This new engineering sample is a small bridge rescue challenge, not the paid What Is a Problem? opener.
🌉🧸
Engineers solve problems by asking what is needed, building a model, testing it, and making it better.
📋 NGSS Alignment:
K-2-ETS1-1 / K-2-ETS1-2 — Ask questions, define a simple problem, and develop a model to solve it.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will define a bridge problem, build a paper bridge for a small toy, test it, and improve one feature.

💡 The Big Idea

Engineers solve problems by asking what is needed, building a model, testing it, and making it better.

🔬 Key Vocabulary

engineerproblembridgemodeltestimprove

📦 Materials

  • 2 cups or blocks
  • Paper strips
  • Tape optional
  • Small toy or counting bear
  • Crayons
  • Worksheet

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Define the problem
A toy needs to cross a gap between two cups without falling.
Step 2 — Name the goal
The bridge should hold the toy for three seconds.
Step 3 — Build version 1
Use one paper strip as a bridge.
Step 4 — Test
Place the toy gently and count to three.
Step 5 — Improve
Fold, layer, or tape the paper to make version 2.
Step 6 — Share evidence
Student says what changed and whether it worked better.

💬 Discussion Questions

Who needs help in this problem?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.
What should the bridge do?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.
What did you change to improve it?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.

🚀 Extension

Try this next: Ask your child to repeat one part of the test tomorrow or with one changed material, then explain what stayed the same and what changed.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Teaser purpose: This sample is intentionally complete enough to teach today while leaving the paid unit sequence intact.
  • Evidence move: Press students to point to a mark, drawing, comparison, or observation before they explain.
  • Family-friendly pacing: If attention is short, do steps 1–4 today and the worksheet discussion later.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

What success looks like

A strong answer names the problem, the goal, and one improvement backed by test evidence.

  • Student names the important observation or test result.
  • Student uses unit vocabulary in a simple sentence.
  • Student connects evidence to the science idea instead of guessing.
