🚗 Motion Changes with Force 🚗

Physical Science — Forces & Motion | Unit 1, Lesson 2 of 6

📚 Grade: 3rd Grade ⏱️ Duration: 40–50 minutes ⚡ Subject: Physical Science
📋 NGSS Standard:
3-PS2-1 — Plan and conduct an investigation to provide evidence of the effects of balanced and unbalanced forces on the motion of an object.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will test how different strengths of pushes change motion and use evidence to explain how stronger or weaker forces affect how far an object moves.

💡 The Big Idea

A force can change motion. A stronger push can make an object move farther or faster. A weaker push may move it only a little. Scientists compare results and use evidence from repeated tests.

💡 Key vocabulary: force, motion, distance, stronger force, weaker force, evidence, compare

📦 Materials

  • 1 toy car, small block, or object that slides
  • 1 ramp made from cardboard/book (optional) or flat table surface
  • Masking tape
  • Ruler or measuring tape
  • Pencil
  • Printed worksheet

🔬 Investigation

Step 1 — Set a Starting Line (5 min)
Use tape to mark a starting line. Place the object at the same spot each time.
Step 2 — Try a Gentle Push (8 min)
Give the object a gentle push. Measure how far it moved. Record the distance.
Step 3 — Try a Stronger Push (8 min)
Push the same object harder from the same starting line. Measure again and compare.
Step 4 — Repeat and Compare (10 min)
Do each kind of push at least twice. Look for patterns in your results.

💬 Discussion Questions

🗣️ Question 1: "What happened when the push was stronger?"

💬 A stronger push often made the object travel farther or faster.

🗣️ Question 2: "Why do we repeat the test?"

💬 Repeating helps us check if the pattern happens again and gives us better evidence.

🗣️ Question 3: "What is your evidence?"

💬 Students should use their measured distances or observations from the tests.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Consistency matters. Keep the object and starting point the same for each test.
  • Students do not need perfect measurements. The goal is comparing stronger and weaker forces clearly.
  • Encourage students to use actual numbers or comparative words like farther, less far, faster, slower.

Part 2

  • When the push was stronger, the object moved farther / moved faster.
  • Evidence should include measured distances or clear observations from repeated tests.

What Success Looks Like

  • Student compares stronger and weaker pushes correctly.
  • Student uses evidence from the test table to explain motion changes.
