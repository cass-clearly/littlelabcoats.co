Improving the Design

Engineering — Design Process | Unit 1, Lesson 5 of 6

📚 Grade: 3rd Grade⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes🛠️ Subject: Engineering — Design Process
🔁📈🪛
Engineers redesign by changing the part that evidence shows needs improvement.
📋 NGSS Standard:
3-5-ETS1-3 — Plan and carry out fair tests in which variables are controlled and failure points are considered to identify aspects of a model or prototype that can be improved.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will revise version 1 into version 2 by making an evidence-based improvement and predicting how it should work better.

💡 The Big Idea

Redesign is not starting over randomly. Engineers use evidence from testing to decide what should change in the next version.

🔬 The Science

After a test, engineers study which part of the design did not do its job well enough. They then make a targeted change instead of changing everything at once.

In this unit, students might add more padding, strengthen a weak side, center the fragile object more carefully, or reduce unnecessary weight. The best redesign is the one that responds directly to the failure point.

Version 2 is stronger when students can explain the reason for the change. This lesson prepares them to justify the redesign before defending it publicly in Lesson 6.

Let's Get Started

Step 1 — Review the Failure Point: Re-read the evidence from Lesson 4 and identify the most important weakness to address.
Step 2 — Plan One Targeted Change: Choose one improvement that connects directly to the weak part of version 1.
Step 3 — Build Version 2: Create the revised prototype using the planned change.
Step 4 — Predict the Improvement: State what should work better in version 2 and why.
Step 5 — Compare Version 1 and Version 2: Explain how the redesign responds to the evidence from testing.

Discussion Questions

Why should version 2 connect to version 1 evidence?Look for answers like: Because redesign should fix a real weakness, not be random.
Why is it helpful to change one key part at a time?Look for answers like: It makes it easier to tell what actually improved the result.
What kind of change might help a weak side support?Look for answers like: Adding stronger support, more tape, or better balance.
Why should engineers predict what version 2 will do better?Look for answers like: It helps them connect the change to a reason and expected result.
How is redesign different from starting over with no plan?Look for answers like: Redesign uses evidence from the earlier test.

Extensions

Extension: Make a before/after sketch of version 1 and version 2 with labels for the changed parts.

Extension: Ask a family member whether your redesign makes sense and what evidence they want to hear.

Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching note: Keep redesign focused. Too many changes at once make the evidence harder to use.

Teaching note: Use sentence frames if students struggle to explain why a change should help.

✂️ PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

1️⃣ What Needed to Improve? — Sample Use

Accept the failure point the student observed in Lesson 4 if it is specific and evidence-based.

2️⃣ What Will I Change? — Sample Ideas

Possible responses include adding more padding, strengthening a side wall, changing where the object sits, or improving how pieces are attached.

3️⃣ Predict Version 2 — Model Response

“Version 2 should work better because I added stronger side supports where version 1 bent during the drop.”

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Student identifies a real failure point from version 1.
  • Student proposes a targeted redesign.
  • Student predicts why the redesign should improve the result.

🛠️ Troubleshooting / Teaching Notes

  • If the redesign change is random, ask which exact weakness it is supposed to fix.
  • If the student says version 2 is better only because it is newer, ask what evidence supports that claim.
