🌤️ What Is Weather? 🌤️

Earth Science — Weather & Seasons

📚 Grade: Kindergarten ⏱️ Duration: 30–45 minutes 🔬 Subject: Earth Science — Weather & Seasons 🔢 Lesson: 1 of 6
☀️ 🌧️ 🌬️ ❄️
What's the sky doing right now? That's weather!
📋 NGSS Standard:
K-ESS2-1 — Use and share observations of local weather conditions to describe patterns over time.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will identify at least 4 types of weather and record daily weather observations for one week using a Weather Watcher Journal.

🔬 The Big Idea

Weather is what the atmosphere (the air around Earth) is doing right now — is it sunny? Rainy? Windy? Cold? Weather can change from hour to hour and day to day.

Climate is different — it's the average pattern of weather for a place over many, many years. Denver, Colorado has a semi-arid climate, which means it's usually dry — but any one day could still be rainy or snowy!

💡 Key vocabulary: weather, climate, atmosphere, observation, temperature, precipitation

📦 Materials

🏠 What You Need

  • Paper and crayons
  • Window with a view outside OR safe outdoor access
  • Weather Watcher Journal (last pages of this lesson)
  • Pencil or marker

🔬 Let's Get Started

Step 1 — Go Observe! (5–10 min)
Head to a window or go outside briefly. Ask your child:
  • 👀 What do you see in the sky? (clouds, sun, rain, snow?)
  • 🤚 What do you feel? (warm, cold, wind on your skin?)
  • 👂 What do you hear? (wind, rain drops, birds?)
Let them soak it in like a real scientist would!
Step 2 — Make Your Weather Icons (10 min)
On a blank piece of paper, draw 5 weather icons together: ☀️ sunny, ⛅ cloudy, 🌧️ rainy, 🌬️ windy, ❄️ snowy. Color them brightly. These become your key for the weather journal!
Step 3 — Day 1 Journal Entry (5 min)
Fill in the Day 1 box on the Weather Watcher Journal (next page). Draw the weather icon that matches today. Write or dictate one sentence: "Today the weather is ______."
Step 4 — Continue All Week
Each morning this week, look out the window and fill in the next day's box. By Friday you'll have a week of real weather data! You'll use this journal again in Lesson 6 to look for patterns.
Step 5 — Weather vs. Climate Discussion (5 min)
Read or explain: "Weather is what's happening outside RIGHT NOW. Climate is the pattern over many years — it tells us what weather to usually expect in a place."
Ask: "If Denver usually gets 300 sunny days per year, but it's raining today — is Denver's climate sunny or rainy?"

💬 Discussion Questions

🗣️ Talk about these together:
  • What types of weather have you seen this week?
  • What's your favorite type of weather? Why?
  • What would you wear outside today? Does the weather change what you wear?
  • Big Question: What's the difference between weather and climate?

💡 Suggested answer: Weather is what's happening outside right now — sunny, rainy, windy. Climate is the average weather pattern over many years for a place. Denver, Colorado has a "semi-arid climate" — usually dry — but any given day could be rainy or snowy. Weather changes daily; climate changes over decades.

🚀 Extensions

  • 🌡️ Temperature Check: If you have an outdoor thermometer, record the temperature each day too!
  • 📺 Weather Forecast: Watch a TV or online weather forecast together. What do meteorologists predict for tomorrow?
  • 🗺️ Weather Around the World: Look up what the weather is today in a faraway place like Alaska, Florida, or Australia. How is it different from yours?
  • 📚 Read Aloud: Cloudy With a Chance of Meatballs by Judi Barrett — a wild take on weather!

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • This is a week-long activity: The Weather Watcher Journal is meant to be filled in each day this week. Save it — you'll need it for Lesson 6!
  • Weather vs. climate confusion is normal: Young kids often think "it's raining, so my place has a rainy climate." Use local examples they know.
  • Outdoor safety: Even a peek out the front door counts if it's too cold or wet to go outside longer.
  • Assessment: Can your child name at least 4 types of weather? Can they explain why today's weather might be different from yesterday's?
  • SAVE the journal — it's used again in Lesson 6: Weather Patterns Over Time.

🔑 PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Weather Types — Reference List

  • ☀️ Sunny — clear sky, bright sunlight, warm feeling
  • ⛅ Cloudy — gray or white clouds covering the sky
  • 🌧️ Rainy — water falling from clouds as droplets
  • 🌬️ Windy — air is moving, you can feel it on your skin or see leaves moving
  • ❄️ Snowy — water falls as frozen ice crystals (snow)
  • ⛈️ Stormy — strong wind, heavy rain, often thunder and lightning
  • 🌫️ Foggy — tiny water droplets in the air make it hard to see far

Weather vs. Climate — Key Distinction

  • Weather = what the atmosphere is doing RIGHT NOW (can change day to day, even hour to hour)
  • Climate = the average pattern of weather over many years for a specific place
  • Example: Denver's climate is semi-arid (usually dry), but any one day could be rainy, snowy, or sunny

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Child can name at least 4 types of weather (sunny, cloudy, rainy, windy, snowy)
  • Child completed at least 3 of 5 journal days
  • Child can explain (in their own words) that weather is what's happening now, and climate is the long-term pattern
