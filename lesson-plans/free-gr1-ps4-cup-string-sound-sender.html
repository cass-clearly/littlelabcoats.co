🎯 Learning Objective

Students will build a simple cup-and-string phone, observe vibrations, and explain how sound can carry a message.

💡 The Big Idea

Sound starts with vibrations, and vibrations can carry information from one place to another.

Sound travels through vibrations

The diagram makes the invisible idea visible: voice vibrations move the cup, the string, and the listening cup.

  • Build the sender
  • Compare loose and tight string
  • Connect vibration to message

📦 Materials

  • 2 paper cups
  • String or yarn
  • Paper clip or tape
  • Pencil for adult hole-starting
  • Partner
  • Worksheet

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Build the sender
With adult help, connect two cups with string.
Step 2 — Send a whisper
One person speaks softly into a cup while the other listens.
Step 3 — Change string tension
Try loose string and tight string.
Step 4 — Feel for vibration
Gently touch the cup or string while someone speaks.
Step 5 — Record evidence
Mark which setup carried the message best.
Step 6 — Explain
Student connects vibration to communication.

💬 Discussion Questions
