## Calvin verdict on current Grade 2

Grade 2 already has enough raw files to look busy, but not enough distinct coverage to feel broad, commercial, and progression-safe. The biggest problem is not "too little science overall" so much as "too much ecology repetition and too little fresh Grade 2-only lift". Weather depth is shallow, life cycles are largely absent, matter depth is front-loaded then dropped, and the standalone engineering lane is real but narrow. The next build should **not** add more ecosystem/pollinator/food-chain lessons until the missing weather, life-cycle, and matter arcs exist.

## What is already solid enough to keep

### Keep: Grade 2 Engineering Unit 1 (`gr2-ets1-unit1-*`)
Why it earns its slot:
- Distinct from Kindergarten generic problem-solving and Grade 1 biomimicry tools
- Clear progression into criteria, constraints, testing, and redesign
- Strong standalone sellability as a bridge challenge

Calvin note:
- This proves Grade 2 engineering exists.
- It does **not** mean Grade 2 has enough engineering variety yet.

### Keep, but do not over-expand right now: Grade 2 Physical Science Unit 1 (`gr2-ps1-unit1-*`)
Why it mostly works:
- Each lesson has a different hands-on move: sort, property tests, sink/float, mixing, heating/cooling, foil-boat challenge.
- It is more advanced than Kindergarten physical science and clearly different from Grade 1 sound/light.

Calvin note:
- The unit introduces states of matter well, but states/matter-change depth is still lighter than Serena wants.
- The best next move is a **second matter-focused unit or upgrade lane**, not more small edits to the existing first unit.

## Where Grade 2 is currently weak or repetitive

### Weakness 1: weather is too thin for Grade 2
Current repo state:
- Kindergarten already covers what weather is, wind, water cycle, seasons, animal responses, and weather patterns.
- Kindergarten also has `k-ess3-unit2-lesson5-weather-hazards.html` for safety basics.
- Grade 2 only has one weather lesson inside Earth Science:
  - `gr2-ess1-unit3-l5-weather-patterns.html`

Why this is a real gap:
- Current Grade 2 weather work is mostly a 5-day journal plus weather/climate vocabulary.
- That is too close to Kindergarten `k-ess2-unit1-lesson6-weather-patterns.html`, just with slightly older wording.
- There is no substantial Grade 2 lane for weather tools, clouds, weather events, severe weather comparison, forecasting, or evidence-based storm reasoning.

### Weakness 2: plant and animal life cycles are missing as a real Grade 2 arc
Current repo state:
- Kindergarten has a simple plant life cycle wrap-up in `k-ls1-unit2-lesson6-plant-life-cycle.html`.
- Grade 1 has parent/offspring and inheritance coverage, including `gr1-ls3-unit2-lesson5-plant-parenting.html` and animal-offspring needs/protection/teaching.
- Grade 2 life science is heavily ecosystem-focused, not life-cycle-focused.

Why this is a real gap:
- There is no Grade 2 unit that asks students to compare stages, life-cycle strategies, metamorphosis, timing, or how life stages change needs.
- Serena specifically called this out, and the repo audit confirms it.
- This is exactly the kind of bridge content Grade 2 should own before Grade 3 goes deeper on life-cycle patterning.

### Weakness 3: ecology duplication is already crowding Grade 2
Current repo state:
- `gr2-ls2-unit1-lesson2-pollinators.html` and `gr2-ls2-unit2-l2-plants-partners.html` overlap heavily.
- `gr2-ls2-unit1-lesson4-food-chains.html` and `gr2-ls2-unit2-l3-food-chains.html` are effectively the same concept lane.
- Unit 1 Lesson 6 and Unit 2 Lesson 6 both culminate in design/solution thinking around habitats/ecosystems.

Calvin action:
- Do **not** keep expanding ecosystem coverage until missing units are built.
- Treat Grade 2 LS2 Unit 2 as the main caution flag for repetition.

### Weakness 4: engineering exists, but the fun is too concentrated in a few places
Current repo state:
- Standalone engineering unit: bridge builders
- Embedded engineering experiences: foil boat, erosion barrier, habitat design

Calvin verdict:
- There **is** engineering already, so Serena’s note does not mean "none exists."
- But the current mix is still not enough variety for a strong commercial Grade 2 catalog.
- A second engineering unit is justified because the existing engineering is mostly structure/build stability. Grade 2 still lacks weather-tool design, storm-safety design, and invention-style engineering with stronger science transfer.

## Grade 2 expansion plan — missing units worth building

Order here is based on sellability + distinctiveness + Serena’s explicit concerns.

### 1) Highest-value now: Grade 2 Earth Science Unit — `Weather Detectives & Wild Storms`
Why this is needed:
- Directly addresses Serena’s strongest gap callout
- Gives Grade 2 a real weather identity instead of one thin journal lesson
- Easy to market: families love weather, storms, tools, and forecasting

How it differs from K and Grade 1:
- Kindergarten = name weather, observe patterns, basic safety
- Grade 1 = sky/sun/moon/star patterns, not weather systems/events
- Grade 2 build = use tools, gather stronger evidence, compare event types, explain why different weather events matter, and make predictions from data

Planned 6-lesson arc:
1. **Weather Station Builders**
   - Why needed: gives Grade 2 the instrument-and-data lift missing from K
   - Distinct from earlier grades: not just "what weather do you see?" but "what data can you collect and compare?"
   - Hands-on hook: build a windsock + rain gauge + data board
2. **Cloud Detectives & Condensation Clues**
   - Why needed: adds actual sky-reading science instead of only weather labels
   - Distinct from earlier grades: focuses on evidence from clouds and condensation, not just weather words
   - Hands-on hook: condensation-on-a-can test + cloud matching lab
3. **Severe Weather Case Files**
   - Why needed: current repo has no Grade 2 severe-weather comparison lane
   - Distinct from earlier grades: compares event types, warning signs, and impacts instead of basic safety-only talk
   - Hands-on hook: tornado bottle + storm case-file sorting
4. **Forecast Like a Meteorologist**
   - Why needed: gives a real reasoning step between observing and predicting
   - Distinct from earlier grades: uses multi-day evidence instead of simple tomorrow guesses
   - Hands-on hook: forecast board + choose-the-best-prediction challenge
5. **Water in the Air**
   - Why needed: ties weather to evaporation, condensation, clouds, and precipitation with stronger reasoning
   - Distinct from earlier grades: moves past K water-cycle introduction into evidence-based explanation of why water changes form in weather
   - Hands-on hook: bag-water-cycle plus warm/cold surface comparison
6. **Engineer for the Storm**
   - Why needed: gives the weather unit a strong commercial capstone and more fun engineering variety
   - Distinct from earlier grades: Grade 2 students apply weather evidence to a design, not just build any shelter
   - Hands-on hook: test a mini storm-safe shelter or storm alert tool against fan/water spray conditions

### 2) Next missing science lane: Grade 2 Life Science Unit — `Life Cycles, Growth & Survival`
Why this is needed:
- Direct Serena callout
- Genuine repo hole between K plant life cycle basics and Grade 3 life-cycle comparisons
- Commercially strong because butterflies, frogs, chicks, seeds, and growing animals are highly parent-friendly

How it differs from K and Grade 1:
- Kindergarten = one simple plant-cycle overview
- Grade 1 = parent/offspring resemblance and care
- Grade 2 build = compare stages, timing, metamorphosis, stage-specific needs, and how life stages affect survival

Planned 6-lesson arc:
1. **Every Life Cycle Has Stages** — hook: build a stage wheel with three organisms
2. **Plant Life Cycle Investigators** — hook: dissect fruit/seed evidence and map seed-to-seed loop
3. **Metamorphosis Lab** — hook: butterfly vs frog compare-and-model cards
4. **Animal Babies Don’t Stay Babies** — hook: growth-sequence strips and size-change evidence
5. **Life Stages Change Needs** — hook: compare what eggs/larvae/juveniles/adults each need to survive
6. **Build a Life Cycle Museum** — hook: trifold or circular exhibit with evidence labels

### 3) Matter hardening lane: Grade 2 Physical Science Unit — `Matter in Action`
Why this is needed:
- States of matter currently appear, but the grade-level story still feels short of Serena’s note
- Strong companion product lane for kitchen science families

How it differs from existing Grade 2 PS1 Unit 1:
- Existing unit = classify properties, mixtures, heating/cooling, boat challenge
- New unit = deeper evidence on state changes, reversible/irreversible changes, gases doing work, and particles-as-models without becoming too abstract

Planned 6-lesson arc:
1. **Matter Moves and Changes** — hook: solid/liquid/gas evidence stations
2. **Gases Do Work** — hook: balloon rocket and air-pressure pushes
3. **Melting, Freezing, Evaporating, Condensing** — hook: four-process lab board
4. **Kitchen Chemistry vs Physical Change** — hook: compare melting chocolate, toast, whipped cream, popped popcorn
5. **Which Change Can We Undo?** — hook: claim-evidence sorting game
6. **Engineer a Temperature Keeper** — hook: design a mini cooler or ice protector

### 4) Additional engineering unit is justified: Grade 2 Engineering Unit 2 — `Weatherwise Builders`
Why it is justified even though engineering already exists:
- Current standalone engineering catalog for Grade 2 is bridge-only
- Embedded design lessons are good, but they are not enough variety for a strong paid catalog
- This unit would let Grade 2 apply weather, material, and measurement learning in a new design context

How it differs from K and Grade 1:
- Kindergarten = general problem solving
- Grade 1 = biomimicry and simple model improvement
- Grade 2 new lift = compare competing designs, test against weather constraints, and justify changes with evidence

Planned 6-lesson arc:
1. define the weather problem
2. compare shelter/tool ideas
3. plan with criteria and constraints
4. build first prototype
5. test in wind/rain simulation
6. redesign and defend the better version

## Recommended build order after this audit

1. **Weather Detectives & Wild Storms**
2. **Life Cycles, Growth & Survival**
3. **Matter in Action**
4. **Weatherwise Builders**

Reason:
- Weather is the most obvious current hole and the easiest immediate sell.
- Life cycles are the next clearest true missing lane.
- Matter hardening is valuable, but Grade 2 already has one decent PS1 unit.
- Additional engineering is justified, but it will be stronger after weather science exists to feed it.
