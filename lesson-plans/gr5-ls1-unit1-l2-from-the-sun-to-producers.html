From the Sun to Producers

Life Science — Ecosystems & Food Webs | Unit 1, Lesson 2 of 6

📚 Grade: 5th Grade⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes🔬 Subject: Life Science — Ecosystems & Food Webs
☀️🌿
Producers capture the Sun’s energy and support the whole web.
📋 NGSS Standard:
5-LS2-1 — Develop a model to describe the movement of matter among plants, animals, decomposers, and the environment.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will explain how producers capture energy from the Sun and support the rest of the ecosystem.

💡 The Big Idea

Plants are producers because they use sunlight to make food. That stored energy becomes available to other organisms.

🔬 The Science

The Sun is the original energy source for most ecosystems on Earth. Plants and algae use sunlight to make sugars through photosynthesis.

When animals eat plants, they gain access to some of that stored energy. That is why producers are such an important part of a food web.

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Identify producers (5–10 min)
Sort organism cards into producers and non-producers.
Step 2 — Trace the energy source (10 min)
Draw arrows from the Sun to a plant and then to an animal.
Step 3 — Explain photosynthesis simply (10 min)
Describe how a plant uses sunlight to make food.
Step 4 — Model the dependency (10 min)
Students make a mini diagram showing why animals depend on producers.
Step 5 — Reflect on ecosystem importance (10 min)
Discuss what would happen without producers.

💬 Discussion Questions

Why are plants called producers?They make their own food using sunlight.
Where does most ecosystem energy begin?With the Sun.
How do animals depend on producers even if they do not eat plants directly?Predators depend on prey that ultimately depend on producers.

🚀 Extensions

Extension: Find an example of a producer in a pond, forest, or ocean ecosystem.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: Keep photosynthesis age-appropriate: sunlight helps plants make food. No need to overcomplicate the chemistry.
