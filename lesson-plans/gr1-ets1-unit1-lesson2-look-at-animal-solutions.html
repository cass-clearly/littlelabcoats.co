Little Lab Coats

🦆 Look at Animal Solutions 🦔

Engineering Design — Unit 1, Lesson 2 of 6

📚 Grade: 1st Grade⏱️ Duration: 30–40 minutes🧪 Subject: Engineering Design
🪶🦷🦶
Nature is full of structures that solve problems.
📋 NGSS Standards:
1-LS1-1 — Use materials to design a solution to a human problem by mimicking how plants and/or animals use their external parts to help them survive, grow, and meet their needs.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will observe animal parts that solve problems and describe how those parts could inspire human-made designs to solve a human problem.

💡 The Big Idea

Animals have body parts that help them solve problems. Ducks have webbed feet for swimming. Hedgehogs have spines for protection. Birds have beaks that help them get food. Engineers can look at these structures and borrow ideas from nature.

💡 Key vocabulary: mimic, structure, protect, move, carry, solve

📦 Materials

  • Pencil and crayons
  • Worksheet pages
  • Optional pictures of ducks, birds, turtles, hedgehogs, burrs, or seed pods

🔬 Let's Get Started

Step 1 — Notice Animal Parts (8 min)
Look at a few animal examples together. Ask: "What body part do you notice?" and "What job does it do?"
Step 2 — Match Structure to Job (8 min)
Talk about a few examples:
  • Webbed feet help animals swim.
  • Claws help animals dig or climb.
  • Beaks help birds pick up food.
  • Spines help animals protect themselves.
Step 3 — Borrow the Idea (8 min)
Ask: "How could a person use an idea like that?" Example: webbed hands for swimming gloves, a claw-like grabber tool, a protective helmet inspired by shells. Remind your child they do not need a perfect or realistic invention—just a clear nature idea that could help a person.
Step 4 — Draw a Nature-Inspired Idea (8 min)
Have your child draw one animal part and one tool or object inspired by it.

💬 Discussion Questions

🗣️ Question 1: "What problem does this animal part help solve?"

💬 It might help with moving, getting food, staying safe, or carrying seeds.

🗣️ Question 2: "How could a human tool copy that idea?"

💬 A good answer explains what job the tool would do, not just what it looks like.

🗣️ Question 3: "Why do engineers look at nature?"

💬 Nature already has many useful solutions. Engineers can learn from them.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes:
  • This lesson bridges life science and engineering.
  • Children do not need advanced biomimicry language; "copying an idea from nature" is enough.
  • Strong student outcome: naming one animal structure and one matching human solution idea.

🔑 PARENT/TEACHER ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Accept any sensible pairing of animal structure + job + human design idea.

Sample pairing: Duck feet help with swimming → swimming gloves that help a person move through water.
