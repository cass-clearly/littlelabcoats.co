Deposition Builds New Landforms

Grade 4 Earth Science — Shaping Earth’s Surface | Unit 1, Lesson 3 of 6

📚 Grade: 4th Grade⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes🔬 Subject: Earth Science — Shaping Earth’s Surface
🏖️🌊🌀
When moving water or wind slows down, dropped sediment can build new landforms.
📋 NGSS Standard:
4-ESS2-1 — Make observations and/or measurements to provide evidence of the effects of weathering or the rate of erosion by water, ice, wind, or vegetation. / 4-ESS2-2 — Analyze and interpret data from maps to describe patterns of Earth’s features.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will explain how deposition happens and identify evidence that sediment was dropped and collected in a new place.

💡 The Big Idea

Deposition happens when moving water, wind, or ice drops the sediment it was carrying. This can build new landforms over time.

🔬 The Science

Weathering makes sediment. Erosion moves it. Deposition is the settling step that happens when the moving force loses energy.

Sandbars, deltas, and piles of sediment at the bottom of slopes are all examples of deposition.

Students should connect cause and effect: when movement slows, carrying power decreases, so sediment is left behind.

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Mix sediment in water (8 min)
Stir a small amount of sediment into water and notice that it stays suspended for a while.
Step 2 — Let the water slow down (10 min)
Pour carefully into a tray or let the container rest and watch sediment settle.
Step 3 — Observe where material collects (10 min)
Notice thicker piles, layers, or new shapes where sediment was dropped.
Step 4 — Connect to real places (10 min)
Compare the model to river deltas, sandbars, beaches, and piles at the base of hills.
Step 5 — Explain the sequence (10 min)
Students describe weathering, erosion, and deposition in order using one example.

💬 Discussion Questions

What causes deposition to happen?The moving water, wind, or ice slows down and drops sediment.
How is deposition connected to erosion?Erosion carries material; deposition is where that carried material gets dropped.
What evidence shows deposition in your model?Students may mention piles, layers, or sediment settled at the bottom.
Can deposition build landforms?Yes. Repeated deposition can build deltas, beaches, and other features.

🚀 Extensions

Extension: Look at photos of river deltas or sandbars and compare them to your model.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: Ask students to narrate the full sequence: break, move, settle.

Misconception to catch: Students may think sediment disappears when it leaves one place. Emphasize that it often collects somewhere else.
