Water on Earth Systems Reference Card

Grade 5 · ESS1
Earth’s water moves among the ocean, land, air, and living things in connected systems driven by the Sun and gravity.
Key vocabulary
water cycleevaporationcondensationrunoff
What to notice
  • Most of Earth’s water is in the ocean.
  • The Sun drives evaporation.
  • Gravity moves water downhill as runoff and into groundwater.
  • Water systems connect weather, land, and living things.
Materials snapshot
  • Clear cup or jar
  • Warm water and ice
  • Map or globe
  • Tray, soil, and spray bottle
  • Worksheet and pencil
Talk-about prompts
  • Where is most of Earth’s water found?
  • How do the Sun and gravity move water?
  • What is the difference between runoff and groundwater?
  • How do humans depend on Earth’s water systems?
Why it matters

Understanding Earth’s water systems helps students explain weather, erosion, water resources, and why actions in one part of the system can affect another.
