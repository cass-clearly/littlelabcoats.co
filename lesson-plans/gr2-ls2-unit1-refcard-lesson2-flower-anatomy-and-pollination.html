Flower Anatomy & Pollination

Grade 2 · Unit 1
Pollen starts on the anther, pollinators carry it, and it needs to land on the stigma so the flower can make seeds.
Key vocabulary
anther stigma pollen sepal petal pollinator
What to notice
  • Petals are the colorful parts that help attract pollinators.
  • Sepals are the small outer parts under the petals that help protect the flower bud.
  • Anthers hold the pollen.
  • Stigma is the sticky part in the center that catches pollen.
Mini-lab steps
  • Look at the flower from the outside first.
  • Find petals, then look underneath for sepals.
  • Look for yellow dust on the anthers.
  • Point to the stigma in the center before starting the worksheet.

Anther

Stigma
Sticky part that catches pollen
How pollination works
  • Pollen starts on the anther.
  • A bee, butterfly, moth, or bird brushes against it.
  • The pollinator carries pollen to another flower.
  • Pollen lands on the stigma, and later the flower can make seeds.
Good family prompts
  • Which part is bright and easy to see?
  • Which part seems dusty or powdery?
  • Which part is in the center waiting to catch pollen?
  • How does this help the worksheet make more sense?
Try this next: Use a cotton swab, finger, or fuzzy pipe cleaner to pretend to be a pollinator. Touch the anther first, then touch the center of another flower and explain the path out loud: anther → pollinator → stigma.
