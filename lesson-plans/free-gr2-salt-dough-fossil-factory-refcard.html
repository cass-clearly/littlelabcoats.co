Salt Dough Fossil Factory Reference Card

Grade 2
Fossils are clues about long-ago living things, and our salt dough model helps us see how imprints can preserve evidence.
Key vocabulary
fossilimprintevidencepaleontologist
What to notice
  • A fossil can be a shape, print, or preserved remain.
  • Our dough model is faster than real fossil formation.
  • Scientists use fossils as evidence about the past.
  • Different objects leave different patterns and clues.
Materials snapshot
  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Small shells/leaves/toys
  • Tray or baking sheet
Talk-about prompts
  • What kind of clue did your fossil model preserve?
  • How is a real fossil different from our dough model?
  • What could a scientist learn from an imprint?
  • Why do fossils count as evidence?
Why it matters

Fossils work like clues in a mystery. Even when scientists do not have the whole organism, a print, track, or shell shape can reveal what a living thing looked like, how it moved, or where it lived long ago.
