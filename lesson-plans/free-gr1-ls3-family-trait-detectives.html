🐶🔍 Family Trait Detectives

Grade 1 Free Unit Sample — 1-LS3 · Parents & Offspring

📚 Grade: Grade 1🔬 Domain: Life Science⏱️ Time: 30–45 minutes
Free standalone unit teaser: This original sample uses animal-family evidence cards instead of reusing the paid You Look Like Your Parents opener.
🐶🔍
Young animals and plants often look like their parents, but they are not exact copies.
📋 NGSS Alignment:
1-LS3-1 — Make observations to construct an evidence-based account that young plants and animals are like, but not exactly like, their parents.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will compare parent and offspring picture cards to identify shared traits and individual differences.

💡 The Big Idea

Young animals and plants often look like their parents, but they are not exact copies.

🔬 Key Vocabulary

parentoffspringtraitsimilardifferentevidence

📦 Materials

  • Printed or drawn animal family cards
  • Crayons
  • Scissors optional
  • Worksheet
  • Family photo optional only if comfortable

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Choose a family card
Look at one parent animal and one offspring animal.
Step 2 — Circle shared traits
Find color, body covering, ear shape, or pattern similarities.
Step 3 — Mark differences
Find a difference such as size, spots, or shade.
Step 4 — Compare another family
Repeat with a different animal or plant family.
Step 5 — Sort claims
Decide which claims are supported by picture evidence.
Step 6 — Write account
Student completes a claim-evidence sentence.

💬 Discussion Questions

What trait does the offspring share with the parent?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.
What is one difference?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.
How do you know from the picture evidence?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.

🚀 Extension

Try this next: Ask your child to repeat one part of the test tomorrow or with one changed material, then explain what stayed the same and what changed.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Teaser purpose: This sample is intentionally complete enough to teach today while leaving the paid unit sequence intact.
  • Evidence move: Press students to point to a mark, drawing, comparison, or observation before they explain.
  • Family-friendly pacing: If attention is short, do steps 1–4 today and the worksheet discussion later.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

What success looks like

A strong answer says offspring share some traits with parents but are not exactly the same, and names specific observed traits.

  • Student names the important observation or test result.
  • Student uses unit vocabulary in a simple sentence.
  • Student connects evidence to the science idea instead of guessing.
