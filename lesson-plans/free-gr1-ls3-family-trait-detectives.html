Compare parent-and-offspring cards to spot shared traits, differences, and evidence for family resemblance.
Young animals and plants often look like their parents, but they are not exact copies.
📋 NGSS Alignment:
1-LS3-1 — Make observations to construct an evidence-based account that young plants and animals are like, but not exactly like, their parents.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will compare parent and offspring picture cards to identify shared traits and individual differences.

💡 The Big Idea

Young animals and plants often look like their parents, but they are not exact copies.

Shared-trait detective board

The parent/offspring cards make the evidence visible without implying every baby is a perfect copy of its parent.

  • Notice visible traits
  • Compare parent and offspring
  • Use evidence, not guessing

🐾 Animal Cards for This Lesson

Use these cards for the investigation and worksheet. Students can point to the cards on screen, or print this page and cut them apart. The goal is to notice evidence: young animals are like their parents, but not exactly the same.

Animal family 1 — Dogs

🐕
Parent dog
  • Brown fur
  • Floppy ears
  • White chest patch
  • Long tail
🐶
Puppy
  • Brown fur
  • Floppy ears
  • Small white chest patch
  • Shorter body
Detective clue: Same fur color and ear shape; different size.

Animal family 2 — Ducks

🦆
Parent duck
  • Flat bill
  • Webbed feet
  • Smooth feathers
  • Larger body
🐥
Duckling
  • Small flat bill
  • Webbed feet
  • Fluffy down
  • Smaller body
Detective clue: Same bill and feet; different body covering and size.

Bonus plant family — Sunflowers

🌻
Parent plant
  • Tall stem
  • Green leaves
  • Yellow petals
  • Large flower head
🌱
Young plant
  • Green stem
  • Small leaves
  • No flower yet
  • Much shorter
Detective clue: Same green plant parts; different size and flowers.

Extra challenge — Rabbits

🐇
Parent rabbit
  • Long ears
  • Soft fur
  • Big back feet
  • Larger body
🐰
Baby rabbit
  • Shorter long ears
  • Soft fur
  • Small back feet
  • Smaller body
Detective clue: Same ears and fur; different size.

Print tip: keep cards on the page for pointing, or cut each rounded rectangle apart for sorting.

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Choose Animal family 1
Look at the parent dog and puppy cards above.
Step 2 — Circle or point to shared traits
Find color, body covering, ear shape, foot shape, bill shape, or pattern similarities on the built-in cards.
Step 3 — Mark differences
Find a difference such as size, fluffiness, flower, spots, or shade.
Step 4 — Compare Animal family 2
Repeat with the parent duck and duckling cards. If your child wants more practice, use the rabbit cards or the bonus plant family.
Step 5 — Sort claims
Decide which claims are supported by picture evidence.
Step 6 — Write account
Student completes a claim-evidence sentence.

What success looks like

A strong answer says offspring share some traits with parents but are not exactly the same, and names specific observed traits.
