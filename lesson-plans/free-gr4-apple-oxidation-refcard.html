Apple Browning Reference Card

Grade 4
Apple browning is oxidation, and a fair test helps us compare which liquid slows that change best.
Key vocabulary
oxidationcontrolvariablehypothesis
What to notice
  • Start with the control slice: it shows what browning looks like with no treatment at all.
  • Only one factor should change in a fair test.
  • Acidic liquids often slow browning more than plain water.
  • Good data comes from careful comparison over time.
Materials snapshot
  • Apple slices
  • Small bowls
  • Liquids to test
  • Labels
  • Observation chart
Talk-about prompts
  • Which slice changed fastest?
  • How did the control help you compare the other slices?
  • What was your variable?
  • What conclusion can you make from the data?
Why it matters

A strong experiment needs an anchor. The control slice tells students what happens naturally, so every other treatment can be judged against real evidence instead of guesses.

Try this next: Try lemon juice, water, and another kitchen liquid, then compare the slices after 10, 30, and 60 minutes.
