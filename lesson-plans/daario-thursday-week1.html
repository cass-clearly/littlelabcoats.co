👂 Retell Challenge 10 min

Listening — Comprehension & Retelling

Time to be a super listener! 🎧 A parent will read a short passage aloud to you. Your job: listen carefully, then retell the story in your own words with as many details as you can remember!

  1. Sit comfortably and listen — no peeking at the passage! 🙈
  2. When the reading is done, retell what you heard in your own words.
  3. Try to include: who, what happened, where, and why it matters.
  4. Parent scores your retelling using the rubric on the worksheet.
💡 Listening Tip: Picture the story in your head like a movie while you listen. It helps you remember more details!
🧑‍🏫 Parent Note: Read the passage below at a normal pace. Read it only once. After the retelling, you can ask 1–2 prompting questions if needed (e.g., "What happened next?" or "Who was the story about?").

👂 Retell Challenge — Parent Reading Passage

⚠️ FOR PARENT ONLY — Read aloud, do not show to student

Last summer, a boy named Marco found a baby turtle on the beach. The tiny turtle was trying to reach the ocean, but it kept going the wrong way — toward the parking lot instead of the water. Marco knew that baby sea turtles follow the brightest light, and the streetlights were confusing this little one.

Marco carefully picked up the turtle and carried it closer to the waves. He set it down gently on the wet sand. The turtle seemed to know what to do next. It crawled quickly toward the water, and a small wave carried it out to sea.

Marco's mom said that volunteers sometimes come to the beach at night to help baby turtles find the ocean safely. Marco decided he wanted to be one of those volunteers when he got older. He smiled all the way home.

🔤 Word Sort 10 min

Word Analysis — Prefixes, Suffixes & Root Families

Cut out the word cards below and sort them into groups! 🗂️ Look at the beginnings (prefixes), endings (suffixes), and root words to figure out which words belong together.

  1. Cut out all 15 word cards.
  2. Sort them into 3 groups (5 words each).
  3. Tape or write them in the sorting boxes on the worksheet.
  4. Discuss: What do the words in each group have in common?
💡 Word Detective Tip: Look for parts you recognize! un- means "not," re- means "again," and -ful means "full of."

📏 Perimeter Walk 10 min

Measurement — Perimeter & Area

Grab a ruler or tape measure — you're going to measure real things! 📐 Pick a book, a table, or even a whole room and find out how big it really is.

  1. Choose 2 objects to measure (a book + a table, or a table + a room).
  2. Measure all the sides using inches or feet.
  3. Calculate the perimeter (add all sides together).
  4. Calculate the area (length × width).
  5. Compare: Which one is bigger? By how much?
✅ Example — Measuring a Book

Length: 10 inches    Width: 8 inches

Perimeter: 10 + 8 + 10 + 8 = 36 inches

Area: 10 × 8 = 80 square inches

💡 Math Tip: Perimeter is the distance around something (like walking around it). Area is how much space it covers (like painting the top).

✏️ Opinion + Reason 5 min

Shared Activity — Opinion Writing

What do YOU think? 🤔 Pick a topic and share your opinion — but here's the rule: you need 2 good reasons to back it up!

✅ Example

I think recess should be longer because kids need time to run around and get energy out, and it helps us focus better when we go back to class.

💡 Writing Tip: Use this frame: "I think ___ because ___ and ___." Fill in the blanks and you've got a great opinion sentence!
