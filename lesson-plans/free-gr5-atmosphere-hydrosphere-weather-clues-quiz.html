✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Prompt: Use one observation from the tiny weather system to explain how the hydrosphere and atmosphere interacted.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Look for evidence language: Good answers mention the actual droplets, fogginess, warm water, cold lid, or arrows in the model.
  • Model quality matters: Students should name the two systems and show a sequence, not just define vocabulary.
  • NGSS target: The quiz supports 5-ESS2-1 by checking whether the student can use a model to describe Earth-system interaction.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

Detach note: Student wording may vary if the Earth-systems interaction is scientifically correct and evidence-based.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. 1. The hydrosphere
  2. 2. The atmosphere
  3. 3. Evaporation
  4. 4. Condensation
  5. 5. Droplets formed on the cool cover
  6. 6. It shows water moving/changing between systems
  7. 7. Dew, fog, clouds, or precipitation
  8. 8. It is smaller and simpler than the real atmosphere

Short Response Guidance

  • Student names both hydrosphere and atmosphere.
  • Student uses a real observation as evidence.
  • Student explains evaporation and/or condensation accurately.
  • Student connects the model to a real weather clue without overclaiming.
