✍️ Short Response — Defend Your Claim

Prompt: Explain why the ice-cooled lid usually creates stronger weather clues in the model. Use at least two observations as evidence and include the words evaporation and condensation.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Look for model reasoning: Strong answers describe what changed, what evidence appeared, and why cooling matters.
  • Watch for misconceptions: Students may say droplets were already on the lid or that “steam” became water. Push them toward precise language about water vapor cooling.
  • NGSS target: Students should use a model to explain an Earth-system interaction, not just define the atmosphere and hydrosphere.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

Detach note: Wording can vary as long as the science is accurate and evidence-based.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. 1. It cools water vapor faster, causing more condensation.
  2. 2. The lid temperature
  3. 3. Water evaporated from the warm water and condensed on the cooled surface.
  4. 4. Both jars had the same amount of warm water.
  5. 5. It moves heat and moisture, which can change droplet patterns.
  6. 6. It is smaller and simpler than the real atmosphere.
  7. 7. Fog on a window, dew on grass, or cloud formation.
  8. 8. Timed observations from both jars.

Short Response Guidance

  • Student uses at least two real observations from the investigation.
  • Student correctly explains evaporation and condensation.
  • Student identifies why the cooled lid changes the outcome.
  • Student writes in complete thoughts rather than isolated definitions.
