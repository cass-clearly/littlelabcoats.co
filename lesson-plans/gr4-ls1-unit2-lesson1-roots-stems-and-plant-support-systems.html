The Big Idea

Plants may not move from place to place, but they still need strong support systems. Roots hold a plant in place and help it take in water and nutrients. Stems help hold leaves, flowers, and fruit up where they can do their jobs.

When we study plant structure and function, we ask the same kind of question we asked in the animal unit: What job does this structure do for the organism? Roots and stems are strong examples because their jobs are essential to survival and growth.

The Science

Roots usually grow into the soil, where they anchor the plant and absorb water and dissolved nutrients. Stems help support the plant body and connect the roots to the leaves and flowers. Some stems stay soft and green, while others become woody and strong, but in each case the structure supports plant survival and growth.

Key vocabulary: root, stem, anchor, absorb, support, nutrient, transport, structure

Materials

Gather Before You Start

  • printed lesson pages and Student Worksheet
  • small plant, weed, celery stalk, or plant diagram
  • cup of water if observing stems
  • pencil or colored pencils

Let's Get Started

Step 1: Begin with a plant observation. Ask students to point to the roots and stems and predict what each structure does.
Step 2: Discuss why a plant needs to stay upright and connected from roots to leaves.
Step 3: Compare what happens above the soil and below the soil. Roots gather resources below; stems support and connect above.
Step 4: Have students complete a table that matches each structure to its main job and evidence clue.
Step 5: End with a short claim explaining how roots or stems support plant survival and growth.

Discussion Questions

Q: Why would a tall plant need strong stems?Look for support, keeping leaves in the light, and holding flowers or fruit up.
Q: Why are roots important even though we often cannot see them?Guide students toward anchoring and resource uptake.
Q: How do roots and stems work together?Roots take in resources; stems connect and support the rest of the plant.

Extensions

Pull a weed or inspect a kitchen herb and compare the hidden root system to the visible stem system. Talk about which part helps the plant below ground and which part helps above ground.

Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching note: Keep the focus on structure-function reasoning, not on memorizing every plant part. Students should be able to explain how roots and stems help a plant survive and grow.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

Detach note: Student wording may vary if the structure-function science is correct.

  • What Success Looks Like: Student explains that roots anchor and absorb, while stems support and connect the plant body.
  • Acceptable examples: Roots help keep the plant from falling over; roots take in water; stems hold leaves and flowers up; stems connect roots to the rest of the plant.
  • Coach for precision: If a student says “roots hold food,” redirect to water/nutrient uptake and anchoring unless they are discussing storage roots with clear evidence.
