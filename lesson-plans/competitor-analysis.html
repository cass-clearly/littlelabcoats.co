🔬 Competitor Review Analysis

What homeschool parents love and hate about the most popular elementary science curricula — and where the gaps are.
Compiled February 12, 2026 · Clearly Curriculum · Internal Research

📋 Executive Summary

We analyzed reviews of 6 major competitors across Amazon, Reddit r/homeschool, Teachers Pay Teachers, Cathy Duffy Reviews, Homeschool.com, and homeschool blogs.

The recurring pain points: ugly/dated design, heavy material prep, screen dependency, piecemeal coverage, and the secular-vs-religious divide.

🎯 Nobody offers a complete, NGSS-aligned, hands-on, secular, homeschool-designed curriculum with modern design and solved materials management. That's our lane.

Real Science Odyssey

$45–75/subject No NGSS Secular
Pandia Press · Hands-on, literature-based · Covers Bio, Chem, Astro, Earth · PDF or print · Level 1 = K-5

✅ What Parents Love

  • Truly hands-on — every lesson has a lab/activity with household materials
  • Covers evolution, geological time, Big Bang — genuinely secular
  • Goes deep: periodic table, cell theory, genetics at elementary level
  • Well-structured Read → Explore → Absorb → Learn format
  • Background info for parents so they can actually teach it

❌ What Parents Hate

  • "Cheap looking and cluttered" — visual design doesn't match price
  • $45-55 for a PDF feels expensive. Print editions even more.
  • Significant prep time despite "open and go" marketing
  • One subject per year only — no multi-topic survey option
  • Consumable workbook pages — must re-buy for each child
  • No video/multimedia — all text in a video-first world

Mystery Science

Free – $99/yr NGSS ✓ Secular
Video-based lessons built around mystery questions · Originally for classrooms, massively adopted by homeschoolers · Free tier available

✅ What Parents Love

  • "My kids love Mystery Science! The lessons totally capture their attention"
  • Professional, engaging video quality — Doug Peltz is warm and curious
  • True open-and-go — minimal parent prep
  • NGSS-aligned with good assessments
  • Free tier is genuinely useful for supplementing
  • Hooks reluctant learners through video format

❌ What Parents Hate

  • "I just don't want to always do video based" — screen time concerns
  • "Kind of a pain gathering things every time. I just hate prepping as we go."
  • Material packs sized for 30 students — wasteful for homeschoolers
  • Not deep enough as standalone curriculum
  • Feels "school-ish" — group prompts don't work with 1 child
  • Subscription model — content disappears if you stop paying
  • Activities are simple demos, not real experiments
  • Topics feel random and disconnected unit-to-unit

Elemental Science

$60–100+/yr No NGSS Neutral
Classical education approach (Well-Trained Mind) · Living books + experiments + notebooking · Cathy Duffy Top Pick · 10,000+ users

✅ What Parents Love

  • "The best curriculum is the one that gets used!" — actually gets done
  • Flexible 2-day or 5-day scheduling options
  • Classical education alignment — endorsed by Susan Wise Bauer
  • Notebooking creates tangible portfolio of learning
  • "My children begged for science and more science today"
  • Full K-12 progression available

❌ What Parents Hate

  • "Demonstrations don't always make sense" — disconnect from reading
  • "Neutral" on origins — satisfies neither secular nor religious families
  • Requires buying separate "spine" books (DK encyclopedias) — added cost
  • Text-heavy for young kids — reluctant readers struggle
  • Workbook pages uninspiring — basic coloring and labeling
  • Less hands-on than marketed — more read-and-write focused

Guest Hollow

Free – $35 No NGSS Secular
One-person passion project · Living books + hands-on · Free schedules online · Secular · Strongest in middle/high school

✅ What Parents Love

  • Free or very affordable — full outlines available at no cost
  • Living books approach — Charlotte Mason friendly
  • Creative experiments (DNA extraction from strawberries)
  • Detailed day-by-day schedules reduce decision fatigue
  • Responsive creator with genuine homeschool-parent perspective
  • Multi-age friendly unit study approach

❌ What Parents Hate

  • Dependent on library having specific books — breaks down without them
  • One-person operation — slow updates, uncertain longevity
  • Materials look homemade — not visually polished or confidence-inspiring
  • Elementary science offerings are thin — more curated list than curriculum
  • No formal assessments or documentation tools
  • No standards alignment (no NGSS)

Science in the Beginning

$40–50/yr No NGSS Young-Earth Creationist
Jay Wile (PhD Nuclear Chemistry) · Organized around Genesis creation days · Dominates Christian homeschool market · Full-color textbook

✅ What Parents Love

  • Biblical integration feels natural and meaningful for faith-based families
  • Jay Wile's PhD credentials build trust
  • Genuinely interesting experiments with household materials
  • Beautiful, full-color, professional presentation
  • Affordable — full year for ~$40-50, all-in-one
  • Massive community support — Facebook groups, co-op resources

❌ What Parents Hate

  • Young-earth creationism is non-negotiable — impossible to secularize
  • Actively teaches against evolutionary biology
  • Organization by creation days creates odd topic jumps
  • Shallow general survey — doesn't go deep on any one topic
  • Assumes narrow worldview — excludes non-YEC families
  • No digital/video/multimedia component

Teachers Pay Teachers (Top NGSS Sellers)

$5–15/unit · $50–120/bundle NGSS ✓ Varies
Marketplace of individual teacher-created units · Top sellers: Kesler Science, The Trendy Science Teacher, NGSS Science for Kids · Huge variety

✅ What Parents Love

  • Buy exactly what you need — no full curriculum commitment
  • Clear NGSS standards correlation for documentation
  • Top sellers have polished, colorful, modern designs
  • Phenomenon-based, inquiry-driven best sellers (5E model)
  • Reviews visible before purchase — helps with quality filtering
  • Digital + print options available

❌ What Parents Hate

  • No coherent scope & sequence — biggest complaint. You build your own curriculum from pieces.
  • Quality varies wildly — anyone can slap "NGSS" on worksheets
  • Designed for classrooms (25 kids), not homeschool families
  • Many products are worksheet-heavy, not truly hands-on
  • No teacher support or community after purchase
  • Color printing costs add up fast for PDF-only products
  • Copyright restrictions make co-op use expensive

🎯 The 8 Gaps We Can Fill

Recurring pain points across all competitors that define our opportunity

Gap 1: No Complete NGSS + Secular + Hands-On Package

Mystery Science is NGSS but video-dependent. RSO is secular and hands-on but not NGSS. TPT has NGSS units but no scope & sequence. Nobody has all three.
→ Be the first comprehensive, NGSS-aligned, hands-on, secular curriculum designed for homeschoolers.

Gap 2: Beautiful Modern Design at Accessible Price

RSO is "cheap looking and cluttered." Guest Hollow looks homemade. Elemental Science workbooks are "uninspiring." Yet TPT proves parents pay more for good design.
→ Professional, visually appealing materials that match TPT's polish with real curriculum depth.

Gap 3: Open-and-Go Without Screen Dependency

Mystery Science is open-and-go but screen-dependent. RSO and Elemental require significant prep. Parents say: "I don't want to always do video based."
→ Minimal prep, materials lists provided in advance, print-first. No screens required.

Gap 4: Materials Management Solved

"Kind of a pain gathering things every time." Supply lists buried or missing. Material packs sized for 30 students. Mid-lesson scrambles.
→ Clear advance materials lists organized by unit. Common household items. Prep once per unit, not per lesson.

Gap 5: Multi-Age / Multi-Child Friendly

Most curricula are grade-specific and consumable (re-buy for each child). Homeschool families teach siblings together.
→ Units that work across a 2-3 grade band with differentiation notes. Family-friendly licensing.

Gap 6: Depth Without Overwhelm

Mystery Science too shallow. RSO goes deep but one-subject-per-year is limiting. Elemental Science experiments "don't always make sense."
→ Meaningful depth per topic while covering multiple science disciplines per year. Experiments that clearly connect to concepts.

Gap 7: Built-In Assessments & Documentation

RSO, Guest Hollow: no formal assessments. Parents increasingly need documentation for umbrella schools and state requirements.
→ Portfolio pages, rubrics, optional quizzes. Make documentation easy without making it feel like "school."

Gap 8: The "Missing Middle" — Pro-Science, Not Anti-Religion

Jay Wile dominates religious but is anti-evolution. Elemental Science's "neutral" satisfies nobody. Secular options can feel exclusionary to moderate religious families.
→ Teach scientific consensus accurately. "Here's what the evidence shows" — respect families' freedom to add their own perspective.
