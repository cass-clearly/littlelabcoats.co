🕘🌞 Shadow Clock Mini Lab

Grade 1 Free Unit Sample — 1-ESS1 · Sun, Moon & Stars

📚 Grade: Grade 1🔬 Domain: Earth & Space Science⏱️ Time: 30–45 minutes
Free standalone unit teaser: This original sky-patterns sample uses shadow evidence instead of reusing the paid Sun lesson.
🕘🌞
The Sun’s position in the sky appears to change, and shadows can help us observe that daytime pattern.
📋 NGSS Alignment:
1-ESS1-1 / 1-ESS1-2 — Use observations of the Sun, Moon, and stars to describe patterns.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will trace an object’s shadow at two times and use evidence to notice that shadows change during the day.

💡 The Big Idea

The Sun’s position in the sky appears to change, and shadows can help us observe that daytime pattern.

🔬 Key Vocabulary

Sunshadowpatternobservedirectiondaytime

📦 Materials

  • Toy or pencil standing upright
  • Paper
  • Sunny spot
  • Crayon
  • Clock or timer
  • Tape

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Set the marker
Stand a toy or pencil on paper in a sunny spot.
Step 2 — Trace shadow 1
Trace the shadow and label the time.
Step 3 — Wait
Return later the same day if possible.
Step 4 — Trace shadow 2
Trace the new shadow and label the time.
Step 5 — Compare
Look for changes in length or direction.
Step 6 — Explain pattern
Student says how the shadow evidence shows daytime change.

💬 Discussion Questions

How did the shadow change?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.

🚀 Extension

Try this next: Ask your child to repeat one part of the test tomorrow or with one changed material, then explain what stayed the same and what changed.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Teaser purpose: This sample is intentionally complete enough to teach today while leaving the paid unit sequence intact.
  • Evidence move: Press students to point to a mark, drawing, comparison, or observation before they explain.
  • Family-friendly pacing: If attention is short, do steps 1–4 today and the worksheet discussion later.
