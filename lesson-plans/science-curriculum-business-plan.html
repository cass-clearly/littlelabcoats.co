Product Structure — Kindergarten NGSS Curriculum

The full Kindergarten year maps to 36 weeks (1 lesson/week), organized into 6 units of 6 lessons each. Each unit aligns to a specific NGSS Disciplinary Core Idea and integrates Engineering Design (K-2-ETS1) throughout rather than as a standalone unit — this mirrors how real scientists work.

K-PS2 · Forces & Interactions

Unit 1: Pushes, Pulls & How Things Move (Weeks 1–6)

Performance Expectations: K-PS2-1 (plan and conduct investigation on effects of different strengths/directions of pushes & pulls) · K-PS2-2 (analyze data to determine if design solution works)

  • L1
    The Great Push & Pull Sort — Classify objects/actions as pushes or pulls. Sort cards + physical objects (toy car, wagon, door). Materials: sort cards (print), toy car, string, small objects.
  • L2
    Ramp Racers — Investigate how ramp height changes how far a ball rolls. Measure with unifix cubes. Materials: cardboard ramp, ball, unifix cubes, data sheet. 🎒 Kit
  • L3
    Strong Push, Gentle Push — Push objects with different forces, observe & record results. Connect to real-world (bowling, soccer). Materials: ping pong ball, cotton ball, blocks, data sheet.
  • L4
    Magnet Magic — Explore pushes and pulls you can't see. Test which materials are magnetic. Materials: magnets, paperclips, coins, fabric, plastic items, recording sheet. 🎒 Kit
  • L5
    Wind Wagon Engineering Challenge — Design a sail-powered wagon that moves with wind (fan/blowing). Iterate & improve. (ETS1 integration) Materials: cardboard, straws, paper, tape, wheels/caps, fan. 🎒 Kit
  • L6
    Marble Run Madness — Capstone: build a marble run using tubes & ramps. Apply all push/pull/force concepts. Record observations. Materials: cardboard tubes, tape, marble, cup.

Kit vs. Digital: Lessons 2, 4, 5 benefit most from curated kits (magnets, wheels, unifix cubes). Others use common household items — digital-only friendly.

K-PS3 · Energy

Unit 2: Sunshine & Energy (Weeks 7–12)

Performance Expectations: K-PS3-1 (make observations to determine effect of sunlight on Earth's surface) · K-PS3-2 (use tools & materials to design a structure to reduce warming by sunlight)

  • L7
    Sun vs. Shade Temperature Hunt — Use thermometers to compare temperatures in sun and shade around the house/yard. Record data. Materials: 2 thermometers, data sheet, clipboard. 🎒 Kit
  • L8
    Black vs. White: Color & Heat — Place black and white paper/fabric in sunlight, measure temperature after 30 min. Materials: black & white paper/fabric, thermometers, data sheet.
  • L9
    Ice Cream in a Bag — Make ice cream to explore energy transfer (thermal energy). Your existing lesson! Materials: cream, sugar, vanilla, ice, salt, zip bags.
  • L10
    Solar Oven S'mores — Build a solar oven from a pizza box; melt chocolate. Connect to renewable energy. Materials: pizza box, aluminum foil, plastic wrap, chocolate, graham crackers, marshmallows.
  • L11
    Design a Shade Structure — Engineering challenge: build a structure that keeps an ice cube from melting in sunlight. (ETS1 integration) Materials: craft sticks, foil, fabric scraps, tape, ice cubes. 🎒 Kit
  • L12
    Sun Journal Wrap-Up — Review sun observations from the unit. Draw and label "what sunlight does." Create mini science poster. Materials: poster paper, crayons/markers, data from prior lessons.

Kit vs. Digital: Thermometers (L7, L8) are the key kit item. Solar oven and ice cream use kitchen supplies. Shade structure kit materials are useful to include.

K-LS1 · From Molecules to Organisms

Unit 3: Living Things & What They Need (Weeks 13–18)

Performance Expectations: K-LS1-1 (use observations to describe patterns of what plants & animals need to survive)

  • L13
    Living or Not? The Great Sort — Sort pictures and objects into living vs. non-living. Establish criteria (grows, needs food/water, reproduces). Materials: sort cards (print), real objects (rock, plant, toy animal, fruit).
  • L14
    Seed Germination Station — Plant seeds in zip bags with wet paper towels. Observe over 2 weeks. Daily drawing journal. Materials: bean seeds, paper towels, zip bags, spray bottle, observation journal. 🎒 Kit
  • L15
    What Do Plants Need? Light Experiment — Grow 3 identical seedlings: one with light, one in dark, one with partial light. Compare over 2 weeks. Materials: 3 cups, soil, seeds, box for dark, data sheet.
  • L16
    Bird Beak Buffet — Your existing lesson! Explore animal adaptations through tool-based feeding simulation. Materials: tweezers, chopsticks, spoons, clothespins, various "foods." 🎒 Kit
  • L17
    Habitat Diorama — Choose an animal, research its needs, build a shoebox habitat. Label food, water, shelter. (ETS1 integration) Materials: shoebox, construction paper, clay, natural materials, labels.
  • L18
    Pet Vet Observation Day — Observe a real pet or visit animals. Record: What does it eat? Where does it sleep? How does it stay safe? Compare to wild animals. Materials: observation sheet, clipboard, colored pencils.

Kit vs. Digital: Seed kit (L14) and beak buffet tools (L16) are perfect kit items. Other lessons use household/craft supplies.

K-ESS2 · Earth's Systems

Unit 4: Weather Watchers (Weeks 19–24)

Performance Expectations: K-ESS2-1 (use & share observations of local weather conditions to describe patterns over time) · K-ESS2-2 (construct argument from evidence for how plants & animals can change environment)

  • L19
    My Weather Station — Set up a daily weather observation routine: temperature, cloud cover, precipitation, wind. Introduce weather tools. Materials: outdoor thermometer, weather journal, wind sock (make from bag + string). 🎒 Kit
  • L20
    Cloud in a Jar — Create a cloud using warm water, ice, and hairspray. Identify cloud types (cumulus, stratus, cirrus). Materials: jar, warm water, ice, hairspray, cloud ID card (print).
  • L21
    Rain Gauge & Water Cycle — Build a simple rain gauge. Model the water cycle in a zip bag taped to a window. Materials: plastic bottle (cut), ruler, zip bag, blue food coloring, tape.
  • L22
    Wild Wind Experiments — Make a pinwheel and anemometer. Test wind strength at different locations. Materials: paper, pin, pencil eraser, small cups, straws, tape. 🎒 Kit
  • L23
    Weather Forecaster for a Day — Use 4 weeks of weather data to spot patterns. Make a 3-day "forecast." Compare to actual weather app. Materials: weather journal (from L19), forecast worksheet.
  • L24
    Severe Weather Safety Plan — Learn about thunderstorms, tornadoes, blizzards (regional). Design a family safety poster. (ETS1 integration) Materials: poster paper, info cards (print), markers.

Kit vs. Digital: Weather station kit (thermometer, wind sock materials, anemometer supplies) is a high-value kit. Rain gauge and cloud jar use household items.

K-ESS3 · Earth & Human Activity

Unit 5: Our Earth, Our Job (Weeks 25–30)

Performance Expectations: K-ESS3-1 (use a model to represent relationship between needs of plants/animals and places they live) · K-ESS3-2 (ask questions about data from maps showing natural hazards) · K-ESS3-3 (communicate solutions to reduce human impact)

  • L25
    Where Do Animals Live & Why? — Match animals to habitats. Discuss what each habitat provides (food, water, shelter, space). Materials: animal/habitat matching cards (print), world map poster.
  • L26
    Trash Audit — Collect 1 day of family trash (clean items). Sort into categories. Calculate what could be reduced, reused, or recycled. Materials: gloves, sorting bins/bags, tally sheet, scale (optional).
  • L27
    Oil Spill Clean-Up Challenge — Simulate an oil spill (vegetable oil + water + feathers) and try to clean it. Discuss impact on animals. Materials: tray, water, vegetable oil, feathers, cotton balls, spoons, dish soap, paper towels. 🎒 Kit
  • L28
    Compost in a Bottle — Build a mini compost column. Observe decomposition over weeks. Materials: 2-liter bottle, soil, food scraps, leaves, spray bottle, observation journal.
  • L29
    Design a Solution: Reduce, Reuse, Redesign — Choose a waste problem, design a solution (reusable lunch wrap, bird feeder from recycled materials, etc.). Build prototype. (ETS1 integration) Materials: recycled materials collection, tape, glue, scissors.
  • L30
    Earth Helpers Pledge & Poster — Create a personal or family "Earth Pledge" poster with specific actions. Present to family. Materials: poster board, markers, stickers, pledge template (print).

Kit vs. Digital: Oil spill kit (L27) is a standout kit item — fun, dramatic, memorable. Other lessons use household/recycled items.

K-2-ETS1 · Engineering Design (Capstone)

Unit 6: Design Lab — Solve It! (Weeks 31–36)

Performance Expectations: K-2-ETS1-1 (ask questions, make observations, gather information about a problem to solve) · K-2-ETS1-2 (develop a simple sketch, drawing, or model to illustrate a solution) · K-2-ETS1-3 (analyze data from testing to determine best solution)

  • L31
    What Is Engineering? — Introduce the engineering design process. Meet real engineers (diverse photo cards). Practice identifying problems around the house. Materials: engineer photo cards (print), problem-spotting worksheet.
  • L32
    Tower Challenge — Build the tallest freestanding tower using limited materials. Sketch design first, build, measure, iterate. Materials: index cards, tape (limited supply), ruler.
  • L33
    Bridge Builder — Design a bridge that holds the most weight between two stacks of books. Test with coins/washers. Materials: popsicle sticks, tape, coins/washers, books, design journal. 🎒 Kit
  • L34
    Egg Drop Protector — Classic engineering challenge: protect an egg from a drop. Sketch → build → test → redesign. Materials: egg, cotton balls, straws, tape, rubber bands, paper, zip bag.
  • L35
    Float Your Boat — Design a foil boat that holds the most pennies without sinking. Data collection + comparison of designs. Materials: aluminum foil, pennies, tub of water, data sheet. 🎒 Kit
  • L36
    My Invention — Final Project — Students identify a real problem in their life and design a solution. Draw blueprints, build model, present to family. Materials: mixed craft/building supplies, presentation rubric, certificate of completion (print).

Kit vs. Digital: Bridge kit (popsicle sticks, washers) and boat kit (foil, pennies) are great kit items. Tower and egg drop use common household supplies.

📦 Materials Summary

Of the 36 lessons, approximately 12 lessons (marked 🎒) have materials that are ideal for a curated physical kit (specialty items like magnets, thermometers, seeds, craft engineering supplies). The remaining 24 lessons use common household items (paper, tape, food items, recycled materials) and are well-suited for digital-only customers with a clear advance supply list.

🚫 Why NOT Other Platforms

  • WordPress + WooCommerce: More flexible but requires technical maintenance. Plugin conflicts, security updates, hosting management. Too much overhead for Serena.
  • Squarespace: Beautiful templates but weak e-commerce. No native subscriptions, clunky digital delivery.
  • Teachable/Thinkific: Great for courses but not physical products. Would need a second platform for kits.
  • Etsy/TPT: Good for discovery but high fees (15–25%), no brand building, no email list ownership. Use as additional sales channel, not primary.

📱 Additional Channels (Phase 2+)

  • Teachers Pay Teachers: List individual units at $18 each. TPT takes ~20% but provides built-in audience of millions. Use as a funnel to your main site.
  • Etsy: List physical kits. Etsy's audience skews toward handmade/curated — kits fit well.
  • Amazon: Future possibility for kits once volume justifies FBA. Complex — Phase 4+.

📦 Batch Production Strategy

  1. Batch by unit: Complete all 6 lessons in a unit before moving to the next. This allows unit-level coherence and scope review.
  2. Cass pre-drafts: Cass can draft all 6 lesson plans in a unit in one session (~2 hours). Serena then reviews and tests in batches.
  3. Photo days: Batch experiment testing into 2–3 "photo shoot" days per unit. Set up, test, photograph multiple experiments in sequence.
  4. Kit sourcing: Once lesson materials are finalized, bulk-order kit components for the unit. Source from Amazon, Dollar Tree, Oriental Trading, ULINE for packaging.
