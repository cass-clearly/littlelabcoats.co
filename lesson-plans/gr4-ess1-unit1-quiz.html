✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Choose one place where Earth’s surface changed in this unit. Explain whether weathering, erosion, deposition, or protection evidence was most important, and cite one observation or measurement that supports your explanation.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Use the short response: It checks whether the student can move beyond vocabulary to evidence-based explanation.
  • Look for precision: Strong answers name the process, cite evidence, and explain how the evidence fits the process.
  • Rigor move: Encourage students to use words like rate, pattern, evidence, compare, and because.

✂️ PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Parent note: Student wording may vary if the science reasoning is correct.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. Weathering
  2. Erosion
  3. Deposition
  4. Wider channels and new sediment piles
  5. Vegetation helps hold soil in place
  6. They make comparisons clearer and more trustworthy
  7. Ice can scrape and push sediment
  8. It compares evidence from more than one tested solution

Short Response — What Success Looks Like

  • Student correctly names a relevant process.
  • Student cites at least one observation or measurement as evidence.
  • Student explains why the evidence supports the claim.
