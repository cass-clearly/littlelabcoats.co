Energy & Motion Reference Card

Grade 4 · PS3
Energy can move from one object to another and cause motion changes that students can observe, compare, and explain with evidence.
Key vocabulary
energymotioncollisionfrictionspeedevidence
What to notice
  • A higher starting position can give the same object more energy and more speed.
  • Collisions can transfer energy and start another object moving.
  • Faster-moving objects often cause bigger motion changes.
  • Rough surfaces create more friction and slow motion sooner.
  • Fair tests change one variable at a time and keep other parts the same.
Materials snapshot
  • Toy cars, marbles, or rolled balls
  • Books or boxes for ramp tests
  • Ruler or tape measure
  • Rough and smooth surfaces
  • Worksheet and pencil
Talk-about prompts
  • What evidence showed that the higher start made the object faster?
  • What evidence shows that energy moved during a collision?
  • How did speed change the result of the test?
  • What did friction do on the rough surface?
  • Which variable did you change, and which did you control?
Why it matters

This unit helps students connect what they can see — higher starts, speeding up, slowing down, colliding, and stopping — to the science idea that energy moves and causes motion changes. Careful evidence and fair comparisons make those explanations stronger.

Try this next: Build a tiny home motion lab with one car, one ramp, and two different surfaces. Ask your child to change one thing at a time and defend the results with evidence.
