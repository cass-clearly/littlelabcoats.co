Surfaces and Friction Change Motion

Physical Science — Energy & Motion | Unit 1, Lesson 4 of 6

📚 Grade: 4th Grade⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes🔬 Subject: Physical Science — Energy & Motion
🧽➡️🧊
Rough and smooth surfaces change how quickly motion slows down.
📋 NGSS Standard:
4-PS3-1 — Use evidence to construct an explanation relating the speed of an object to the energy of that object.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will compare rough and smooth surfaces and explain how friction changes motion.

💡 The Big Idea

Friction between surfaces can reduce motion, and rougher surfaces usually slow objects more quickly.

🔬 The Science

Friction is a force that acts when surfaces rub against each other. Students can observe friction when a moving object slows down sooner on one surface than another.

This lesson keeps the object and starting setup the same while changing only the surface. That helps students explain how friction affects speed and stopping.

Students should connect the visible motion change to the idea that energy is not staying in the same kind of motion the whole time.

📦 Materials

🏠 Household Items to Gather

  • Toy car, marble, or slider
  • One smooth surface such as cardboard or table
  • One rough surface such as towel, felt, or carpet sample
  • Tape measure or ruler
  • Worksheet and pencil

📋 Printables

  • Student worksheet page from this lesson

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Predict the surfaces (5 min)
Ask which surface will let the object move longer and why.
Step 2 — Run the smooth-surface test (10 min)
Use the same start each time and record how far the object moves.
Step 3 — Run the rough-surface test (10 min)
Repeat on the rough surface without changing the object or release method.
Step 4 — Compare stopping behavior (10 min)
Notice which surface slowed the object sooner.
Step 5 — Explain friction (10 min)
Use the evidence to explain how surface type changed the motion.

💬 Discussion Questions

Which surface slowed the object more quickly?The rougher surface usually did.
Why should the starting push or release stay the same?That helps you tell whether the surface caused the change.
What evidence would support a friction claim?Distance traveled, stopping point, or how quickly the object slowed.

🚀 Extensions

Extension: Try a third surface and rank the surfaces from least friction to greatest friction using evidence.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: Let students touch the surfaces and describe them, then connect those descriptions to the motion results.

Misconception to catch: Students may think the object “wanted” to stop. Bring them back to the surface interaction as the cause.

🔑 PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Sample explanation

The rough surface usually slowed the object sooner, which is evidence of greater friction. The smooth surface usually let the object keep moving longer.

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Student compares two surfaces fairly.
  • Student identifies which surface created more friction.
  • Student supports the claim with motion evidence.
