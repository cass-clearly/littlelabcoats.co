Share and Compare Bridge Designs

Engineering — Bridge Builders | Unit 1, Lesson 6 of 6

📚 Grade: 2nd Grade⏱️ Duration: 40–50 minutes🛠️ Subject: Engineering — Bridge Builders
🎤🌉🏁
Engineers explain the problem, the final design, and the evidence that made them trust it.
📋 NGSS Standard:
K-2-ETS1-3 — Analyze data from tests of two objects designed to solve the same problem to compare the strengths and weaknesses of how each performs.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will present a final bridge design and explain how test evidence supports the improvements they made.

💡 The Big Idea

Engineering is not finished when the building stops. Engineers also share what they learned, what changed, and why the final version is stronger.

🔬 The Science

A good final share-out tells the whole design story: the problem, the bridge plan, the test, the weak point, and the redesign.

In this lesson, students compare their final bridge to version 1 and explain which evidence made them trust the redesign more.

When engineers communicate clearly, other people can learn from the design and improve their own ideas too.

Let's Get Started

Step 1 — Retell the Challenge: Students restate the bridge problem and the success rules.
Step 2 — Show the Final Bridge: Present the final design and point out the most important parts.
Step 3 — Compare Version 1 and Version 2: Explain the weak point in version 1 and the key change made for version 2.
Step 4 — Defend the Improvement with Evidence: Use test results to show why the redesign deserves trust.
Step 5 — Celebrate the Engineering Journey: Reflect on what the student would keep or change in a future version.

Discussion Questions

What problem did your bridge solve?Look for: a clear statement about crossing the gap safely.
What was your most important improvement?Look for: one specific feature changed from version 1 to version 2.
What evidence makes you trust your final design?Look for: test results, less sagging, or more weight held.
What would you try next if you made version 3?Look for: a thoughtful next step, not random decoration.

Extensions

Extension: Turn the presentation into a mini poster with a before-and-after drawing of version 1 and version 2.

Extension: Watch someone else share and name one design choice you learned from their bridge.

Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching note: Prompt students to mention both the problem and the evidence, not only the final bridge.

Teaching note: If a student says “It is better because it looks better,” ask what the test showed.

✂️ PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

1️⃣ My Final Bridge Story — Strong Response

Look for a complete explanation that names the bridge problem, the key redesign, and the test evidence supporting version 2.

2️⃣ Draw or Label Your Final Design — Acceptable Features

The drawing or labels should match the final shared bridge and include meaningful parts such as folded sides, braces, or support pieces.

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Student clearly states the bridge problem.
  • Student names one important improvement from version 1 to version 2.
  • Student supports the final design with actual test evidence.
