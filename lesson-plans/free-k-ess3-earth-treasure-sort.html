🌎🪨 Earth Treasure Sort

📋 NGSS Alignment:
K-ESS3-3 — Communicate solutions that reduce human impact on land, water, air, or other living things.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will sort everyday “Earth treasures” by source and choose one helper action that protects land, water, air, or living things.

💡 The Big Idea

People use Earth materials every day, and small helper actions can protect the places living things need.

Earth-treasure sorting mat

The sorting visual helps children connect familiar objects to Earth systems and then choose a concrete care action.

  • Sort resources by source
  • Name how people use them
  • Choose a helper action

🔬 Key Vocabulary

Earthresourcewatersoilprotectreuse

📦 Materials

  • Small clean items or pictures: rock, leaf, cup of water, wooden spoon, paper
  • Sorting mat
  • Crayons
  • Worksheet

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Collect safe treasures
Gather natural items or pictures that represent water, soil/rocks, plants, and air.
Step 2 — Sort by source
Place each item under “from Earth” or “made by people using Earth materials.”
Step 3 — Pick a resource
Choose one treasure and name how people use it.
Step 4 — Find a problem
Ask what could happen if people waste or dirty that resource.
Step 5 — Choose a helper action
Draw one action: save water, reuse paper, pick up litter, protect plants.
Step 6 — Share
Student completes: “I can help Earth by ___.”

💬 Discussion Questions

Which item came from Earth?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.
How do people use this resource?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.
What is one helper action our family can do?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.

🚀 Extension

Try this next: Ask your child to repeat one part of the test tomorrow or with one changed material, then explain what stayed the same and what changed.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • At-home win: This free sample is complete enough to teach today and polished enough to show how a full Little Lab Coats lesson feels.
  • Evidence move: Press students to point to a mark, drawing, comparison, or observation before they explain.
  • Family-friendly pacing: If attention is short, do steps 1–4 today and the worksheet discussion later.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

What success looks like

A strong answer connects a resource to a specific helper action, such as saving water, reusing paper, or keeping litter out of habitats.

  • Student names the important observation or test result.
  • Student uses unit vocabulary in a simple sentence.
  • Student connects evidence to the science idea instead of guessing.
