✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Choose two different plant structures from the unit. Explain how one supports survival or growth and how the other supports reproduction.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Use the quiz and short response together: the multiple-choice section checks recognition, while the short response checks whether the student can support a claim with evidence.
  • Good evidence sounds specific: look for a named plant structure and the job it does.
  • Unit emphasis: strong answers should connect roots, stems, leaves, flowers, seeds, or fruits to survival, growth, or reproduction.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

Detach note: Student wording may vary if the structure-function reasoning is correct.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. Roots help anchor the plant and absorb water.
  2. Leaves help the plant make food using light.
  3. Internal pathways move water through the plant.
  4. Flowers support reproduction.
  5. Seeds protect the young plant.
  6. Fruits can help move or protect seeds.
  7. Evidence should name a structure and explain its job.
  8. Several accurate examples make an argument stronger.

What Success Looks Like

  • Student names two plant structures correctly.
  • Student explains what each structure does.
  • Student includes one example tied to survival or growth and one tied to reproduction.

Sample acceptable short response

“Roots help a plant absorb water and stay in place, which supports survival and growth. Flowers help the plant reproduce because they are part of making seeds. These examples show that plant structures do important jobs.”
