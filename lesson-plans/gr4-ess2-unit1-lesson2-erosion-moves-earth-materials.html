Erosion Moves Earth Materials

Grade 4 Earth Science — Shaping Earth’s Surface | Unit 1, Lesson 2 of 6

📚 Grade: 4th Grade⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes🔬 Subject: Earth Science — Shaping Earth’s Surface
🌧️⛰️➡️
Water, wind, ice, and gravity can move weathered Earth materials from one place to another.
📋 NGSS Standard:
4-ESS2-1 — Make observations and/or measurements to provide evidence of the effects of weathering or the rate of erosion by water, ice, wind, or vegetation. / 4-ESS2-2 — Analyze and interpret data from maps to describe patterns of Earth’s features.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will compare weathering and erosion and explain how erosion moves sediment across Earth’s surface.

💡 The Big Idea

Erosion is the movement of weathered material. Water, wind, ice, and gravity can all carry sediment from place to place.

🔬 The Science

Weathering makes smaller pieces. Erosion transports those pieces. Both processes can shape land over time.

Streams carry sediment downhill, wind can lift and move sand, glaciers drag rock, and gravity pulls loose material down slopes.

Students should use evidence to answer not just what changed, but what process best explains the change.

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Review weathering vs erosion (8 min)
Compare breaking rock with moving rock and sort example cards into the two groups.
Step 2 — Make a sloped surface (10 min)
Tilt a tray with soil or crumbs and predict what water will do.
Step 3 — Add water and observe movement (10 min)
Watch how material moves downhill and where it collects.
Step 4 — Explain the evidence (10 min)
Students identify what moved, what caused the movement, and where the material went.
Step 5 — Compare different erosion agents (10 min)
Discuss how wind, water, ice, and gravity can each move Earth materials in different ways.

💬 Discussion Questions

What makes erosion different from weathering?Erosion moves the material; weathering breaks or changes it.
What evidence shows erosion happened in your model?Students may mention sediment moved downhill or collected in a new place.
Why does slope matter?Gravity helps pull water and loose material downhill.
Can erosion happen slowly?Yes. Even small repeated movement over time changes landforms.

🚀 Extensions

Extension: Watch water after rain and look for tiny places where soil or sand has moved.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: Ask students to point to the material before and after movement so their explanation stays evidence-based.

Misconception to catch: Students may say erosion means any change at all. Push them to include movement from one place to another.

Sample ideas

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Student distinguishes movement from breaking.
  • Student identifies water, wind, ice, or gravity as an erosion agent.
  • Student uses observations from the model as evidence.
