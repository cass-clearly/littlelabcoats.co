👂 Listen & Draw 10 min

Listening — Following Verbal Directions

Time to test those ears! 👂 A parent will read a scene description out loud — no peeking! You draw exactly what you hear. Then compare your drawing to the original description. How close did you get?

  1. Grab a pencil and crayons (colors help!).
  2. Listen carefully as a parent reads the scene description — no repeats!
  3. Draw what you heard on the worksheet.
  4. Compare your drawing to the description. Circle what you got right! ⭐
💡 Listening Tip: Close your eyes while listening. Picture the scene in your mind first, THEN draw. Details matter — size, position, color!

🔤 Context Clue Challenge 10 min

Word Analysis — Context Clues & Word Parts

You're a word detective! 🔎 Read each sentence below. There's a tricky word in bold. Use the clues in the sentence AND the word parts (prefixes, suffixes, roots) to figure out what it means. No dictionary!

  1. Read the whole sentence carefully.
  2. Look at the bold word — do you recognize any parts of it?
  3. Use the rest of the sentence to make your best guess.
  4. Write your definition on the worksheet.
✅ Example

Sentence: The kitten was so playful that it chased its tail for an hour.

Word parts: play + ful (full of)

Context clue: chased its tail for an hour

My definition: full of play, likes to have fun

📏 Estimation Station 10 min

Measurement — Estimating & Measuring

Can you guess before you measure? 🤔 Pick 8 objects from around the house. First estimate (guess!) the measurement, then actually measure to see how close you were. The closer your estimate, the more points you score!

  1. Find 8 objects around the house (grab a ruler, measuring tape, and a kitchen scale if you have one).
  2. For each object, decide: am I measuring length, weight, or capacity?
  3. Estimate first — write it down BEFORE measuring!
  4. Measure for real. How close were you?
💡 Estimation Tip: Use benchmarks! A paperclip is about 1 inch. A water bottle holds about 2 cups. An apple weighs about 6 ounces. Compare objects to things you already know!

🔠 Quiz Each Other 5 min · Shared with Mia

Capitalization — Tricky Rules

Time to stump your sister! ✍️ Each of you writes 3 tricky capitalization questions for the other. Then swap and correct together. Who's the capitalization champion? 👑

  1. Write 3 sentences with capitalization mistakes on purpose (or ask "Should this be capitalized?").
  2. Swap papers with Mia.
  3. Fix each other's sentences, then check your answers together.
✅ Example Questions

• Fix this: "on saturday, we went to denver with aunt maria." → On Saturday, we went to Denver with Aunt Maria.

• Should "president" be capitalized here? "The president spoke on TV." → No — it's not used with a name!

✏️ Story Starter 5 min · Shared with Mia

Writing — Creative Fluency

Ready, set, WRITE! ✍️ Read the first sentence below, then keep the story going for 5 minutes. No stopping, no erasing — just let your imagination fly! 🚀

  1. Read the opening sentence.
  2. Set a timer for 5 minutes.
  3. Continue the story — whatever comes to mind!
  4. When the timer beeps, finish your last sentence and put your pencil down.
💡 Writer's Tip: There are no wrong answers! Silly is great. Weird is great. Scary, funny, magical — it all counts. Just keep writing!
