Engineering: Slow the Erosion

Earth Science Unit 3, Lesson 6

📚 Grade: 2nd Grade ⏱️ Duration: 60 minutes 🔬 Subject: Earth Science: Engineering Design 🧰 Kit Lesson: No
🏗️💧🌿🛡️
Engineers design solutions to protect land from erosion. Can you build a barrier to slow the water?
📋 NGSS Standard:
2-ESS2-1: Compare multiple solutions designed to slow or prevent wind or water from changing the shape of the land.

🎯 Learning Goals

  • Apply knowledge of erosion to design a solution to slow water erosion.
  • Test two different barrier designs and compare their effectiveness.
  • Explain which design worked better and why, using evidence.

💡 Background

Erosion can cause serious problems: it washes away farmland, destroys hillsides, and damages coastlines. Engineers design solutions to slow or stop erosion. Farmers plant grasses and trees on hillsides (plant roots hold soil in place). Engineers build retaining walls, install rocks on slopes, and plant native vegetation. In this lesson, you will design and test your own erosion barrier using craft sticks, leaves, and other household materials.

💡 Key vocabulary: barrier, erosion control, retaining wall, vegetation, slope

📦 Materials

🏠 What You Need

  • A baking pan or cookie sheet (tilt one end up with a book to create a slope)
  • Sand or dirt (2 to 3 cups)
  • Water in a small cup for pouring
  • Craft sticks or toothpicks
  • Leaves, grass clippings, or small sticks
  • Optional: small rocks, aluminum foil pieces
  • Paper and pencil for recording

✅ All household items, nothing to buy!

📖 Vocabulary

barriersomething that blocks or slows movement
erosion controlmethods used to prevent or reduce soil loss by water or wind
retaining walla structure that holds back soil on a slope
vegetationplant life, especially grasses and trees used to protect soil
slopea surface that is not flat but tilted at an angle

🔬 Procedure

Step 1 (10 min): Review and Problem: Remind students of the Lesson 3 erosion experiment. The problem: water is washing away the sand/soil. The engineering challenge: design a barrier to slow or stop the erosion.
Step 2 (10 min): Imagine and Plan Design 1: Brainstorm solutions. Could craft sticks be a retaining wall? Could leaves act like ground cover? Sketch Design 1 on paper.
Step 3 (10 min): Build and Test Design 1: Set up the sand slope in the tilted pan. Build your barrier. Pour the same amount of water as in Lesson 3. Observe and record how much sand moved.
Step 4 (10 min): Improve: What worked? What did not? Plan Design 2 with improvements.
Step 5 (10 min): Build and Test Design 2: Build and test the improved design. Compare to Design 1.
Step 6 (10 min): Wrap-Up: Ask the discussion questions and complete the worksheet.

💬 Discussion Questions

🗣️ Q1: Design 1 let some sand move through. How could you improve it?

Suggested answer: Possible improvements: fill gaps between craft sticks, add a layer of leaves in front of the sticks to catch fine particles, pack rocks on the slope to weigh down the soil, or build a more solid wall with overlapping sticks. Real engineers test multiple designs and refine them.

🗣️ Q2: Farmers plant grass on hillsides to prevent erosion. Why do you think plants help?

Suggested answer: Plant roots grow deep into the soil and hold it together like glue. Leaves and stems also slow the water down as it flows over the surface, giving the water time to soak in rather than run off and carry soil away. Grass roots form a dense mat that can hold even steep slopes.

🚀 Extensions

  • 🌱 Grass Roots Test: Compare erosion on a bare sand slope vs. a slope with a piece of grass sod on top. Which has less erosion?
  • 🌊 Real Erosion Control: Research 'riprap' (large rocks placed on slopes near water) or 'terracing' (steps cut into hillsides). Draw an example.
  • 📚 Book Connection: 'Building on Nature: The Life of Antoni Gaudi' by Rachel Victoria Rodriguez

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Fair test: Use the same amount of water poured from the same height in each test so comparisons are valid.
  • Best designs typically: combine a physical barrier (craft sticks) with a ground cover layer (leaves or grass clippings) that slows water and catches fine particles.
  • NGSS link: 2-ESS2-1 asks students to compare multiple solutions to erosion. Students are doing exactly this by building and comparing two designs.
  • Engineering design process: This is another full cycle of Ask, Imagine, Plan, Create, Test, Improve.
