Build Your First Bridge

Engineering — Bridge Builders | Unit 1, Lesson 4 of 6

📚 Grade: 2nd Grade⏱️ Duration: 40–50 minutes🛠️ Subject: Engineering — Bridge Builders
🧱✂️🌉
Building turns a paper plan into a model engineers can actually test.
📋 NGSS Standard:
K-2-ETS1-2 — Develop a simple sketch, drawing, or physical model to illustrate how the shape of an object helps it function as needed to solve a given problem.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will build a first bridge model that follows their plan and stays focused on the challenge criteria.

💡 The Big Idea

A first model does not need to be perfect. It needs to be close enough to the plan that engineers can learn from it and test it honestly.

🔬 The Science

When engineers build a first version, they are turning ideas into something real enough to observe. That is when hidden problems start to show up.

In this lesson, students use their sketch to build a bridge model with simple materials. Their goal is not decoration. Their goal is a bridge that spans the gap and is ready for testing.

If the first model is imperfect, that is useful. Engineers expect version 1 to teach them something.

Let's Get Started

Step 1 — Gather Materials: Lay out only the materials the bridge plan calls for.
Step 2 — Build Version 1: Students follow their sketch and assemble a first model bridge.
Step 3 — Check the Span: Place the bridge across the gap to see whether it reaches correctly.
Step 4 — Make Small Fixes: Allow simple fixes that keep the build aligned to the original plan.
Step 5 — Record How Version 1 Looks: Students note one part they feel confident about and one part they want to watch during testing.

Discussion Questions

Why is it helpful to build from a plan?Look for: it keeps the build focused and easier to explain.
What makes this version “version 1”?Look for: it is the first model before testing and improving.
What part of your bridge are you most interested in testing?Look for: a real feature the student built.
Why are quick fixes okay before the test?Look for: they help the bridge match the plan enough for a fair test.

Extensions

Extension: Make a quick second sketch showing one change you might try later if version 1 struggles.

Extension: Take a photo of version 1 before testing so you can compare it to version 2 later.

Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching note: Encourage students to keep version 1 simple enough that they can actually improve it next lesson.

Teaching note: If a student wants to restart completely, ask whether a small fix would still let them learn from version 1.

✂️ PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

1️⃣ Version 1 Build Check — Acceptable Answers

Answers should match the student's actual build. Look for honest observations about the span, shape, and chosen materials.

2️⃣ What I Want to Watch During Testing — Strong Thinking

Students should name a real feature, such as folded edges, taped joints, or the middle span, and predict where strength or weakness may appear.

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Student builds a bridge that can be placed across the gap.
  • Student can name the shape and materials used.
  • Student identifies one feature to observe during testing.
