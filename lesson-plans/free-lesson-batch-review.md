### Rachel round-1 review notes

1. **Target:** `lesson-plans/k-milk-magic.html`  
   **Observation:** Kindergarten now has the right active target, but the page still reads denser than the strongest K LLC lessons. The science explanation is clearer than before, yet the worksheet compare table and four-question discussion block still skew a little old for the youngest users.  
   **Expected revision action:** Simplify one worksheet demand and tighten one discussion prompt so the Kindergarten page feels more like guided noticing and less like early-elementary written output.

2. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr1-celery-rainbow.html` and `lesson-plans/free-gr1-celery-rainbow-refcard.html`  
   **Observation:** The lesson idea is strong and visible, but the freebie set should use the same scientific-throughline language across lesson and refcard. Right now the lesson emphasizes xylem and visible transport, while the refcard stays more generic and does not explicitly coach the parent toward the “evidence inside the stem” moment.  
   **Expected revision action:** Tighten refcard wording so it more directly mirrors the lesson’s core evidence move: cut the stem, inspect the colored tubes, and use that as proof that water traveled.

3. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr2-salt-dough-fossil-factory.html` and `lesson-plans/free-gr2-salt-dough-fossil-factory-quiz.html`  
   **Observation:** The lesson correctly distinguishes model vs real fossil, but the quiz still leans heavily on vocabulary recognition. The strongest part of the lesson is understanding that the dough fossil is a model of preserved evidence, not a real fossil.  
   **Expected revision action:** Strengthen one quiz explanation and one answer-key cue so the evidence/model distinction is impossible to miss.

4. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr3-egg-drop-kitchen-challenge.html`, `...-refcard.html`, and `...-quiz.html`  
   **Observation:** Grade 3 is conceptually coherent, but the freebie trio still needs tighter “success criterion” language. The lesson talks about reducing force, the refcard talks about impact and redesign, and the quiz tests the broad idea, but the set could do more to anchor students to a measurable success condition before they build.  
   **Expected revision action:** Add one sharper success-criterion cue across the lesson-support artifacts so the design goal feels testable, not just interesting.

5. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr4-apple-oxidation.html` and `lesson-plans/free-gr4-apple-oxidation-refcard.html`  
   **Observation:** The experiment is well chosen, but the freebie set should lean harder into control/variable language because that is the real intellectual payoff of the page. The current refcard is serviceable, but it underplays the control slice compared with the lesson itself.  
   **Expected revision action:** Revise the refcard so the control is visually or copy-wise elevated as the anchor for a fair comparison.

6. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr5-bread-mold-terrarium.html` and `lesson-plans/free-gr5-bread-mold-terrarium-quiz.html`  
   **Observation:** The lesson has strong gross-but-compelling science, but the quiz answer-key support should do more with decomposition reasoning and not only safety/vocabulary. Right now students could pass the quiz with limited explanation of why mold matters ecologically.  
   **Expected revision action:** Strengthen parent-facing answer-key support so decomposition and nutrient cycling are treated as the core science idea, not an afterthought.

7. **Target:** `K–5 free lesson set`  
   **Observation:** Across the full set, the new quiz shells are closer to LLC parity, but the reference cards are still the lightest-weight artifact in the package. They read as useful, yet not fully at the polish level of the strongest existing printable reference cards.  
   **Expected revision action:** Use the revision pass to make the refcards more explicit, more scannable, and more tied to the exact teaching move of each lesson.

### Margaret round-1 review notes

1. **Target:** `lesson-plans/k-milk-magic.html`  
   **Observation:** The page shell is much stronger now, but the Kindergarten pacing still feels slightly text-forward for a free sample that is supposed to delight instantly. The worksheet and answer key are structurally solid; the friction is in how much the parent has to read before feeling ready.  
   **Expected revision action:** Trim or rebalance one explanatory block near the top so setup feels faster and more family-friendly.

2. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr1-celery-rainbow.html` and `lesson-plans/free-gr1-celery-rainbow-quiz.html`  
   **Observation:** Grade 1 is strong on visual payoff, but the quiz should feel a little more parent-coachable. The short response prompt is good, yet the answer-key support could include one more sample evidence sentence a parent could recognize quickly.  
   **Expected revision action:** Add a concise exemplar sentence in the quiz answer key that models a strong Grade 1 evidence response.

3. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr2-salt-dough-fossil-factory-refcard.html`  
   **Observation:** The refcard has the right content, but the “why it matters” panel is still generic across the set. For Grade 2 especially, the printable card should do more than restate that it belongs to a freebie set.  
   **Expected revision action:** Replace or sharpen the generic why-it-matters copy so it teaches a grade-specific takeaway about fossils as clues.

4. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr3-egg-drop-kitchen-challenge.html` and `lesson-plans/free-gr3-egg-drop-kitchen-challenge-quiz.html`  
   **Observation:** The engineering story is intact, but the compare-versions coaching still seems to live more in the lesson than in the companion artifacts. A busy homeschool parent may look to the quiz answer key for what counts as a strong explanation.  
   **Expected revision action:** Strengthen one answer-key line in the quiz so it explicitly tells parents to look for version-1 vs version-2 evidence, not vague “it worked better” language.

5. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr4-apple-oxidation-quiz.html`  
   **Observation:** The updated quiz shell is much better, but the short response guidance could more clearly direct families toward actual data-table evidence rather than intuition about which liquid “should” work.  
   **Expected revision action:** Tighten the short-response guidance so the expected answer cites observed browning results, not just hypothesis language.

6. **Target:** `lesson-plans/free-gr5-bread-mold-terrarium-refcard.html` and `lesson-plans/free-gr5-bread-mold-terrarium.html`  
   **Observation:** The science theme is excellent, but the refcard should surface the ecological payoff more clearly. The lesson gets there; the card still feels more like a checklist than a memorable takeaway.  
   **Expected revision action:** Make decomposition/nutrient cycling more prominent in the refcard’s quick-scan language.

7. **Target:** `K–5 free lesson set`  
   **Observation:** The set now feels real and reviewable, but polish is uneven across artifact types: lessons are strongest, quizzes are improved, refcards still need the most tightening. That does not block review completion, but it should drive the next revision pass.  
   **Expected revision action:** Prioritize refcard clarity and parent-facing exemplar wording in the next revision chunk before any publish-prep claims.
