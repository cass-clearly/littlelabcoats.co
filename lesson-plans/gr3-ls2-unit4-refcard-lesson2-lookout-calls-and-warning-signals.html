Lookout Calls and Warning Signals Reference Card

Grade 3 · Life Science
A warning signal can help more than one organism survive by spreading information quickly.
Key vocabulary
signalwarningdangerresponse
What to notice
  • Signals spread information.
  • One behavior can affect the whole group.
  • Comparison strengthens the explanation.
Materials snapshot
  • Bell or clap signal, scenario cards, worksheet
Talk-about prompts
  • What changed when the signal was used?
  • Why does speed matter?
  • Could the warning be visual instead of sound?
Why it matters

Warning behavior gives a very clear example of group survival advantage.

Try this next: Notice whether birds or pets ever react to one another’s warning behaviors in everyday life.
