Gravity Points Down

Physical Science — Forces & Motion | Unit 1, Lesson 2 of 6

📚 Grade: 5th Grade⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes🔬 Subject: Physical Science — Forces & Motion
🍎⬇️
No matter the object, gravity pulls toward Earth.
📋 NGSS Standard:
5-PS2-1 — Support an argument that the gravitational force exerted by Earth on objects is directed down.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will gather evidence that gravity pulls objects toward Earth.

💡 The Big Idea

Earth’s gravity always pulls objects downward, even when the objects start from different heights or positions.

🔬 The Science

Gravity is a force that acts at a distance. It pulls objects toward Earth without touching them.

Students test dropped and rolling objects to see that gravity always acts downward, even when objects move in other directions first.

📦 Materials

🏠 Household Items to Gather

📋 Printables

  • Student worksheet page from this lesson

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Predict the direction (5 min)
Ask students to point the direction gravity pulls.
Step 2 — Drop two objects (10 min)
Drop two safe objects from the same height and watch what happens.
Step 3 — Roll then fall (10 min)
Roll an object off a table or ramp safely and observe that it still falls down.
Step 4 — Record evidence (10 min)
Students write what they saw in each test.
Step 5 — Support the argument (10 min)
Students explain how the evidence shows gravity is directed down.

💬 Discussion Questions

What direction did the objects move because of gravity?They moved downward toward Earth.
Did the shape of the object change the direction of gravity?No — gravity still pulled down.
How does a rolling object still show gravity?It can move forward and downward at the same time.

🚀 Extensions

Extension: Try dropping light and heavy safe objects from the same height and compare what is the same about the direction.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: Students do not need advanced math here. Focus on the direction of the force and the observable pattern.

Safety: Use only safe household objects and keep feet clear.
