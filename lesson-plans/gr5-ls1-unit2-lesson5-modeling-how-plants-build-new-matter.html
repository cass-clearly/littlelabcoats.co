Modeling How Plants Build New Matter

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will build a model showing how air and water become part of new plant matter.

💡 The Big Idea

A good model can show that plant growth matter comes chiefly from air and water moving into the plant system.

🔬 The Science

Science Snapshot
Plant growth is the result of matter moving in and being rearranged into living tissue.

What is happening?

Plants build new matter when they use light energy to rearrange water and carbon dioxide into sugars. Those sugars can then be changed into cellulose, starch, oils, and other materials that make up plant tissues.

Why it works that way

This is a conservation-of-matter idea. The plant is not creating matter from nothing; it is taking in materials from the environment and reorganizing them into living structures. That is why careful models show inputs and outputs.

Look for this in the lesson

Students should include arrows from air and water into the plant and labels for where the new matter ends up. A strong model shows that plant growth is matter transfer plus energy use.

Ask while you read: Does your model explain where the plant’s new matter came from before it became part of the plant?

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Review the evidence
Look back at water transport and air-input lessons.
Step 2 — Draw the plant system
Include roots, stem, and leaves.
Step 3 — Add arrows for matter
Show water entering roots and carbon dioxide entering leaves.
Step 4 — Add sunlight separately
Label it as energy, not matter.
Step 5 — Explain the model
Write how the inputs help build new plant material.

💬 Discussion Questions

Why do arrows matter in the model?They show movement of materials into the plant.
Why keep sunlight separate?Because it provides energy, not the material itself.
What does the model explain?Where the plant’s growth matter chiefly comes from.

🚀 Extensions

Extension: Create a 3D plant-input model with paper arrows and labels.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: Require arrows and labels so the model explains process, not just plant anatomy.

🔑 PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Sample model explanation

Water enters the roots, carbon dioxide enters the leaves, and sunlight provides energy. These inputs allow the plant to build new matter for growth.

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Student includes water and air as matter inputs.
  • Student separates sunlight as energy.
  • Student uses the model to explain growth, not just label plant parts.
