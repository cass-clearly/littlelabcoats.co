✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Prompt: Explain how one design choice helped reduce the force on the egg. Use evidence from a test.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Use the quiz and short response together: The multiple-choice section checks recognition, while the short response checks whether the student can explain an idea with evidence.
  • Good evidence sounds specific: Look for references to the actual materials, observations, comparison tests, or redesign notes from the lesson.
  • Freebie parity note: This quiz is intentionally structured like a real LLC companion artifact rather than a bare answer sheet.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

Detach note: This page is for parent/teacher reference. Student wording may vary if the science idea is correct.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. 1. Keep the egg from breaking during impact
  2. 2. The moment two things hit each other
  3. 3. It can absorb or spread out some of the force
  4. 4. Use the result to redesign and improve
  5. 5. The egg cracked on one side when it landed nose-first
  6. 6. It lets you improve using what you learned
  7. 7. A limit on materials, size, or time
  8. 8. Change one major feature and compare the result

What Success Looks Like

  • Impact is when objects hit
  • Padding can absorb or spread force
  • Redesign uses evidence from testing
  • Best evidence: Student compares version 1 and version 2 with a specific result such as “the first design cracked, but the second stayed intact from the same drop height.”

Short Response Guidance

  • Student states a correct science idea from the lesson.
  • Student uses one real observation, comparison, or test result as evidence.
  • Student connects the evidence to the idea instead of only restating a vocabulary word.
  • Look for version-1 vs. version-2 evidence instead of vague statements like “it worked better.”
