Scope label: `Grade 3 LS1 Batch 1 — Lessons 3–6`

### Rachel round-1 review notes

1. **Target:** `gr3-ls1-unit1-lesson3-plant-life-cycles-and-seeds.html`  
   **Observation:** The plant lesson is structurally strong, but the worksheet evidence prompt is too open-ended. Students could describe a seedling without explicitly naming where birth, growth, reproduction, and death appear in the model.  
   **Expected revision action:** Make the evidence prompt explicitly require students to connect their plant model to all four parts of the shared pattern.

2. **Target:** `gr3-ls1-unit1-lesson4-animal-life-cycles-and-metamorphosis.html`  
   **Observation:** The metamorphosis comparison is good, but the worksheet table is a little too shorthand for homeschool use. It would help to ask students for a specific clue from the stages so they justify why a life cycle counts as dramatic change or gradual growth.  
   **Expected revision action:** Add clue-based wording to the worksheet and answer key so students support the metamorphosis judgment with stage evidence.

3. **Target:** `gr3-ls1-unit1-lesson5-comparing-life-cycle-patterns.html`  
   **Observation:** This lesson is the scientific comparison bridge, but the worksheet directions still let students stay generic. The compare task should explicitly require the full pattern language instead of letting “same” mean only one vague similarity.  
   **Expected revision action:** Tighten the compare directions so students name birth, growth, reproduction, and death as comparison evidence.

4. **Target:** `gr3-ls1-unit1-lesson6-model-and-explain-a-life-cycle.html`  
   **Observation:** The capstone lesson needs one extra self-check or revision move so students do not stop after drawing a neat model. The lesson should push one final pass for labels, reproduction, and evidence.  
   **Expected revision action:** Add a quick self-check or revision aid on the worksheet and note it in the answer key / success criteria.

5. **Target:** `Grade 3 LS1 Batch 1`  
   **Observation:** The preview depth is now better aligned to the unit anchors because the new lessons include a Learning Objective before `Materials`. Keep that behavior and avoid any future regression to a shallower top cut.  
   **Expected revision action:** Preserve the learning-objective-through-vocabulary preview boundary while revising content.
