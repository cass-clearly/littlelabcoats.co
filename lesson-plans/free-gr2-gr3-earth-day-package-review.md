# Earth Day free package review — Grade 2/3

## Rachel review notes
- The original lesson framing read more like an internal draft than a release-ready freebie. The opening needed a stronger promise so families immediately understand what they are getting.
- The teaching arc needed one clearer evidence-language move for this age band. Kids were sorting, but the page needed an explicit sentence frame that turns sorting into explanation.
- The answer key needed a faster skim section for adults so the science clarifications are visible without reading every paragraph.
- The local-rules caveat was important, but it needed to be framed as a scientific habit rather than a hedge.

## Margaret review notes
- The lesson needed a gentle, visible “what’s included” feel so a parent can decide to print it quickly the night before Earth Day.
- The footer CTA should feel welcoming, not salesy, and should point to real places on the site.
- The printable sign needed one line explaining where to use it so it feels immediately useful in a home or homeschool setup.
- The package would share better with a dedicated social image instead of relying on the lesson screenshot alone.
