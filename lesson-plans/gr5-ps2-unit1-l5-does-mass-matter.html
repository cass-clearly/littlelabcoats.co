Does Mass Matter?

Physical Science — Forces & Motion | Unit 1, Lesson 5 of 6

📚 Grade: 5th Grade⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes🔬 Subject: Physical Science — Forces & Motion
⚖️🪙
Mass can affect how easily motion changes.
📋 NGSS Standard:
5-PS2-1 — Support an argument that the gravitational force exerted by Earth on objects is directed down.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will test how adding mass can change an object’s motion in a force investigation.

💡 The Big Idea

Objects with more mass can respond differently to the same push, so scientists compare tests carefully and look for evidence patterns.

🔬 The Science

Mass is the amount of matter in an object. When an object has more mass, it may be harder to start, stop, or change its motion.

In this lesson, students compare a lighter and heavier version of the same object using the same ramp or push.

📦 Materials

🏠 Household Items to Gather

📋 Printables

  • Student worksheet page from this lesson

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Make a light and heavy version (5–10 min)
Use the same object and add coins or washers to one version.
Step 2 — Run the same test twice (10 min)
Use the same ramp height or the same push.
Step 3 — Observe differences (10 min)
Record what changed in speed, distance, or stopping.
Step 4 — Check fairness (10 min)
Students explain how they kept the test fair.
Step 5 — Make a claim (10 min)
Students explain how mass affected the results.

💬 Discussion Questions

Why should the ramp stay the same in both tests?Changing only one variable makes the comparison fair.
How did the heavier object behave?It may have needed more force to change in the same way.
What counts as evidence here?Specific observations like distance, speed, or stopping behavior.

🚀 Extensions

Extension: Try adding a third mass level and see whether the pattern continues.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: If students get mixed results, that is okay — focus on careful comparison and evidence, not perfection.
