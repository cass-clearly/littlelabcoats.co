Reference Card — Weathering Breaks Rocks

Grade 4 · ESS2
Weathering is the process of breaking rock into smaller pieces. It can happen slowly through water, ice, temperature changes, and living things.
Key vocabulary
weatheringrocksedimentcracksurfaceevidence
What to notice
  • Wind, water, ice, and roots can slowly break rock into smaller pieces.
  • Use evidence from observations, maps, or models.
  • Connect cause and effect clearly.
Materials snapshot
  • Small rock or sidewalk pebble
  • Cracker or dry bread to model breaking
  • Cup of water
  • Worksheet and pencil
Talk-about prompts
  • What process is happening here?
  • What evidence supports that idea?
  • Is the change fast or slow?
Why it matters

This lesson helps students build stronger explanations about how Earth’s surface changes.
