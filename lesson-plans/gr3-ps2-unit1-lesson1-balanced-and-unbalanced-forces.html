⚖️ Balanced and Unbalanced Forces ⚖️

Physical Science — Forces & Motion | Unit 1, Lesson 1 of 6

📚 Grade: 3rd Grade ⏱️ Duration: 40–50 minutes 🧲 Subject: Physical Science
↔️📦
Forces can cancel out, or one force can win and cause motion.
📋 NGSS Standard:
3-PS2-1 — Plan and conduct an investigation to provide evidence of the effects of balanced and unbalanced forces on the motion of an object.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will investigate what happens when forces are balanced or unbalanced and use evidence from a simple test to explain why an object stayed still or moved.

💡 The Big Idea

A force is a push or a pull. When forces are balanced, they cancel out and the object does not change motion. When forces are unbalanced, one force is stronger, and the object starts moving, stops moving, speeds up, slows down, or changes direction.

💡 Key vocabulary: force, push, pull, balanced force, unbalanced force, motion, evidence

📦 Materials

  • 1 small toy car, block, or smooth object that can slide
  • 1 flat surface (table or floor)
  • 2 index cards or stiff paper pieces
  • Masking tape
  • Pencil
  • Printed worksheet

🔬 Investigation

Step 1 — Observe a Balanced Situation (8 min)
Place the object on the table. Gently press on both sides at the same time with two index cards so the pushes match. Ask: "Did the object move? What do you notice?"
Step 2 — Try an Unbalanced Push (8 min)
Now push harder from one side only. Watch the object move. Ask: "What changed? Which force won?"
Step 3 — Test Again (10 min)
Repeat the test with different strengths of pushes. Try one balanced push and two unbalanced pushes. Record what happened each time.
Step 4 — Explain with Evidence (10 min)
Use your observations to explain why the object stayed still in one test and moved in another.

💬 Discussion Questions

🗣️ Question 1: "What happened when the pushes matched?"

💬 When the pushes matched, the forces were balanced, so the object did not change motion.

🗣️ Question 2: "What happened when one push was stronger?"

💬 When one push was stronger, the forces were unbalanced, so the object moved.

🗣️ Question 3: "What is your evidence?"

💬 Students should point to what they saw happen in their tests, not just repeat a definition.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Keep the investigation physical and visible. The main goal is noticing how motion changes when forces are equal or unequal.
  • Push students to use observation words: stayed still, moved, slid farther, changed direction.
  • This lesson works best when students compare one balanced test to one unbalanced test directly.

🔑 PARENT/TEACHER ANSWER KEY

Part 1

Answers vary. Balanced-force trials should show little or no change in motion. Unbalanced-force trials should show the object moving.

Part 2

  • When the forces were balanced, the object stayed still / did not change motion.
  • When the forces were unbalanced, the object moved / changed motion.

What Success Looks Like

  • Student can tell the difference between balanced and unbalanced situations.
  • Student uses evidence from the investigation to explain motion.
