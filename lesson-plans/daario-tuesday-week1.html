👂 Verbal Instructions 10 min

Listening — Following Multi-Step Directions

Time to sharpen those listening ears! 🎧 A parent will read you a set of instructions — your job is to listen carefully and follow each step without asking them to repeat it.

  1. Round 1: Listen to a 3-step instruction (like making a paper airplane). Do it!
  2. Round 2: Try a 4-step instruction (like setting up a simple game). Trickier!
  3. Bonus Round: Can you handle 5 steps? Give it a shot!
🎯 Sample 3-Step Instruction

Parent reads: "Get a piece of paper, fold it in half the long way, then draw a star on the front."

👆 Daario listens once, then does all 3 steps from memory!

🎯 Sample 4-Step Instruction

Parent reads: "Go to the kitchen, get a cup, fill it halfway with water, then bring it back and set it on the table."

💡 Listening Tip: Try picturing each step in your mind like a movie. That makes it easier to remember!

🔤 Word Building 10 min

Word Analysis — Prefixes & Suffixes

You're a Word Builder today! 🏗️ Start with a base word and see how many real words you can create by adding prefixes (beginnings) and suffixes (endings).

  1. Look at the base word in the center of the tree.
  2. Add prefixes (un-, re-, pre-, dis-) or suffixes (-ing, -ed, -er, -ly, -ful, -ness) to build new words.
  3. Write each new word on a branch. Try to fill as many as you can!
🌳 Example: Base Word = "play"

play → playing, played, player, replay, playful, playfully

That's 6 words from one base! Can you beat that? 💪

Today's base words to try: 🌳 help    🌳 kind    🌳 paint

💡 Word Tip: Not sure if a word is real? Say it out loud in a sentence. If it makes sense, it counts!

📏 Cooking Math 10 min

Measurement — Fractions & Doubling/Halving

Math gets delicious when you're in the kitchen! 🍳 Today you'll practice measuring with cups and tablespoons — and figure out how to double and halve a real recipe.

  1. Read the recipe below. It makes 1 batch of cinnamon sugar toast.
  2. Figure out the amounts if you want to make 2 batches (double it!).
  3. Now figure out the amounts for half a batch (halve it!).
  4. Bonus: Actually make it in the kitchen and practice measuring! 🍞
🧮 Quick Reminder

Doubling: 1/2 cup × 2 = 1 cup   |   Halving: 1 cup ÷ 2 = 1/2 cup

Tricky one: Half of 1/2 cup = 1/4 cup   |   Half of 1 tablespoon ≈ 1½ teaspoons

💡 Kitchen Tip: Use real measuring cups and spoons if you can. Hands-on practice makes fractions click!

🔠 Capitalization Rule of the Week 5 min

Shared — Grammar & Conventions

This week's rule: Proper nouns get capital letters! 🅰️

📏 The Rule

A proper noun is the specific name of a person, place, or thing.

❌ "I went to the store." → store is general (common noun)

✅ "I went to Walmart." → Walmart is specific (proper noun = capitalize!)

More examples: dog → Buddy  |  city → Denver  |  teacher → Mrs. Johnson

Now write 5 sentences that each use at least one proper noun (capitalized correctly!) on the worksheet below.

💡 Trick: If you can replace the word with a different name, it's probably a proper noun. "I went to ___" → Walmart, Target, Costco = proper nouns!
