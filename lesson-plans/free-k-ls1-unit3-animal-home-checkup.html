Animals survive when their basic needs are met in the places they live.
📋 NGSS Alignment:
K-LS1-1 — Use observations to describe patterns of what animals need to survive.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will inspect pretend animal homes and decide whether each animal has food, water, air, and shelter.

💡 The Big Idea

Animals survive when their basic needs are met in the places they live.

Animal-home checklist

A cozy habitat scene turns the abstract word habitat into a concrete checkup: what does the animal need, and is it there?

  • Look for food, water, air, shelter
  • Match needs to habitat parts
  • Explain one missing need

🔬 Key Vocabulary

animalneedfoodwaterairshelter

📦 Materials

  • Toy animal or animal picture
  • Small box, towel, or paper “home”
  • Blue paper or cup for water symbol
  • Pretend food card
  • Crayons
  • Checklist worksheet

🧪 Investigation

Step 1 — Choose an animal
Pick a toy animal or picture and name where it might live.
Step 2 — Build a pretend home
Use a box, towel, or drawing to create a simple habitat.
Step 3 — Check food
Add or draw one food the animal could eat.
Step 4 — Check water and air
Add a water symbol and talk about breathing.
Step 5 — Check shelter
Add a hiding/resting spot.
Step 6 — Explain the checkup
Students tell which need was easiest to forget and why it matters.

💬 Discussion Questions

What does your animal eat?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.
Where can it get water?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.
How does shelter help an animal stay safe?Use evidence from the activity in your answer.

🚀 Extension

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • At-home win: This free sample is complete enough to teach today and polished enough to show how a full Little Lab Coats lesson feels.
  • Evidence move: Press students to point to a mark, drawing, comparison, or observation before they explain.
  • Family-friendly pacing: If attention is short, do steps 1–4 today and the worksheet discussion later.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

What success looks like

A strong answer includes food, water, air, and shelter and explains that missing a need can make the animal unsafe or unable to survive.

  • Student names the important observation or test result.
  • Student uses unit vocabulary in a simple sentence.
  • Student connects evidence to the science idea instead of guessing.
