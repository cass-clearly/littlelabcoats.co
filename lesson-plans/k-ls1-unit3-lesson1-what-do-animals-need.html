🐾 What Do ALL Animals Need? 🐾

Life Science — Animal Needs  |  Unit 3, Lesson 1 of 6

📚 Grade: Kindergarten ⏱️ Duration: 30–45 minutes 🔬 Subject: Life Science — Animal Needs 🌿 Unit: Unit 3, Lesson 1 of 6
🐶 🐠 🐦 🐘
From tiny fish to giant elephants — all animals share four basic needs to survive!
📋 NGSS Standard:
K-LS1-1 — Use observations to describe patterns of what plants and animals (including humans) need to survive.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will NAME and DESCRIBE the four basic needs (food, water, air, and shelter) that all animals share, and give one example of each.

💡 The Big Idea

Every animal on Earth — from the tiniest ant to the biggest blue whale — needs four things to survive. These needs are the same no matter where an animal lives or what it looks like:

🍎 Food
💧 Water
🌬️ Air
🏠 Shelter
💡 Key vocabulary: survive, needs, food, water, air, shelter

📦 Materials

🏠 Gather These Household Items

  • Bowl of water
  • Small snack (crackers or raisins)
  • A hand fan or piece of cardboard (to fan yourself and feel air — air is all around us, not stored in a container!)
  • Small box or container (represents shelter)
  • Stuffed animals or family pet
  • Paper and crayons

🎲 Distractor Items for the Sort

  • Toy car, crayon, coin, book, or any other household objects that are not survival needs

🔬 Let's Get Started

Step 1 — Introduce the Big Question (5 min)
Hold up a stuffed animal (or point to the family pet). Ask: "What does this animal need to stay alive? What would happen if it didn't have food for a week? What about water?"

Let kids brainstorm freely. Write or draw their ideas on paper. Then reveal: "Scientists have figured out that ALL animals need exactly four things to survive. Let's find out what they are!"
Step 2 — Meet the Four Needs (5–8 min)
Gather your four items and introduce them one at a time:
  • 🍎 Food — Hold up the snack. "Animals need food for energy. Without energy, they can't move, grow, or do anything!"
  • 💧 Water — Hold up the bowl. "Every animal needs water. Even animals that live in deserts find ways to get water!"
  • 🌬️ Air — Fan yourself or breathe gently on your hand. Ask: "Can you feel that? What is it?" → Air! "Air is all around us — you can't hold it in a box, but you can feel it. Animals need air — specifically a gas called oxygen — to breathe."
  • 🏠 Shelter — Hold up the small box. "Shelter is a safe place to rest, stay warm, and hide from danger. It could be a den, a nest, a pond, or even a dog bed!"
🌬️ Air is everywhere! Fan yourself with a piece of paper or a hand fan and ask "What do you feel?" (air / wind). Breathe gently on your child's hand — "That's air! It's all around us — animals breathe it wherever they are, not from a container." Great sensory hook for little learners.
Step 3 — Survival Sorting! (10–12 min)
Spread out all your items on the floor: the four survival items (snack, water bowl, hand fan/cardboard, box) AND the distractor items (toy car, crayon, coin, etc.).

Call out items one at a time and ask: "Does a dog need this to survive?"
  • Bowl of water → ✅ YES — survival need (Water)
  • Crackers → ✅ YES — survival need (Food)
  • Fan/cardboard (wave it to feel air) → ✅ YES — survival need (Air — air is everywhere, we just used the fan to FEEL it!)
  • Box → ✅ YES — survival need (Shelter)
  • Toy car → ❌ Nope — fun, but not needed to survive!
  • Crayon → ❌ Nope — same!
  • Coin → ❌ Nope — animals don't use money 😄
Make two piles: NEEDS and NOT NEEDS.
Step 4 — Needs Hunt! (8–10 min)
Go on a "Needs Hunt" around the house! Look for evidence of where your pet (or stuffed animal) gets each need met:
  • 🍎 Food — Where is the pet food bowl or food kept?
  • 💧 Water — Where is the water bowl?
  • 🌬️ Air — Open a window! Air is everywhere — animals breathe it wherever they are.
  • 🏠 Shelter — Where does the pet sleep? Their bed, a tank, a cage?
If no pet: use a stuffed animal! "Where would we put this animal's food bowl? Where would it sleep?" Set it all up in a little pretend habitat corner.
Step 5 — Wrap-Up (3–5 min)
Point to each item on the floor and have kids shout out the four needs in order: "FOOD! WATER! AIR! SHELTER!"
Ask: "Can you remember all four? Let's see if you can say them without looking!"

💬 Discussion Question

🐠 vs 🐕 — Think about this together:

"Do you think a fish needs the same things as a dog?"

Give kids a moment to think and share their ideas before offering the answer below.


💡 Suggested Answer: Yes! Both a fish and a dog need food, water, air, and shelter — but they get those things in different ways.

A fish uses gills to pull the oxygen out of water — the same oxygen we breathe from air, just dissolved in water instead. A dog breathes air directly with lungs and might sleep in a cozy dog bed. Same four needs — very different methods!

Follow-up: "Can you think of another animal? What does IT need?"

🚀 Extensions

  • 🎨 Habitat Diorama: Build a mini habitat for a stuffed animal in a shoebox. Include all four needs: a tiny food bowl, a water cup, a toy "shelter," and air (it's already there — just name it!).
  • 📚 Read Aloud: "What Do Animals Need?" by Lisa J. Amstutz or "Living Things and Their Needs" by Daniel Nunn.
  • 🌿 Nature Walk: Go outside and try to spot evidence of animal needs in the wild — a bird's nest (shelter!), a puddle (water!), berries on a bush (food!)
  • 🔢 Animal Matching Game: Name an animal, name one of its four needs, and describe how it meets that specific need. Take turns!
  • 📖 Unit Preview: "Next time, we're going to look at just ONE need up close — stay tuned!" (Builds anticipation for Lesson 2.)

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • No materials to buy — everything needed is already in your home!
  • Introducing "air": Fan your child's hand or breathe gently on it — ask "what do you feel?" Air is everywhere, not stored in a container. The key concept is that air is all around us; we don't need to capture it in something to show it exists.
  • No pet? Use a stuffed animal as a stand-in. Set up a little "habitat corner" for it with a pretend food bowl, water cup, and bed.
  • Introduce vocab: survive, needs, food, water, air, shelter. Keep coming back to these words throughout the lesson.
  • This is the foundation lesson — all five other lessons in this unit build on these four needs. Getting kids fluent in "food, water, air, shelter" sets them up for everything that follows.
  • Don't worry about perfect sorting — the discussion matters more than accuracy. If a child says "a toy car" is a need, ask them why! It's a window into their thinking.

✅ PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

✅ SURVIVAL NEEDS

  • 🍎 Apples — Food
  • 💧 Bowl of water — Water
  • 🌬️ Air — Air (oxygen) — it's all around us, we can feel it!
  • 🏠 A cozy den — Shelter
  • 🌿 Grass — Food (for herbivores like rabbits, horses, cows)
  • 🪺 Bird's nest — Shelter
  • 🐟 Fish food (flakes) — Food (what you feed a pet fish — a clear survival need!)

❌ NOT SURVIVAL NEEDS

  • 🚗 Toy car — fun for us, not for animals!
  • 🖍️ Crayon — creative, but not survival
  • 🪙 Coin — animals don't use money 😄
  • 📱 Phone — no service in the wild!
  • 🎁 Gift box — nice surprise, but not needed

💡 Discussion Hint Notes

  • 🌿 Grass card: Kids might say grass isn't food — great teaching moment! It IS food for many animals (herbivores). Encourage kids to think about who eats what.
  • 🪺 Bird's nest: Reinforce "shelter" — shelter doesn't have to be a house. It's any safe place to rest and stay protected. Nests, burrows, caves, and dens all count!
  • 🌬️ Air card: Air is everywhere — reinforce that animals don't get air "from a container." Fan your hand or breathe on it: "That's air! It's all around us — animals breathe it wherever they are."
  • Fish and water: Fish also need oxygen to survive — but instead of breathing air like we do, their gills extract dissolved oxygen directly from water. This connects to the Discussion Question about fish vs. dogs.

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Child can name all four needs: food, water, air, shelter (with prompting is fine at this stage!)
  • Child can correctly sort at least 3 of the 4 survival need cards into the NEEDS column.
  • Child can say one thing about how their pet (or favorite animal) gets at least ONE need met.
  • Child can answer the discussion question: "Yes, a fish and a dog need the same things — just differently!"
