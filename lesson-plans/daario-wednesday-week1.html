🔤 Word Analysis — Break It Down 10 min

Vocabulary — Syllables, Roots & Affixes

Big words aren't scary when you break them apart! 🧩 Take each word below, split it into syllables, find the root, and figure out what it means.

✅ Example

Word: unhappiness

Syllables: un • hap • pi • ness

Root: happy   |   Prefix: un- (not)   |   Suffix: -ness (state of)

Predicted meaning: the state of not being happy

Your 5 words:

  1. replay
  2. unfriendly
  3. powerful
  4. disappear
  5. submarine
💡 Clue: Prefixes go at the beginning (re-, un-, dis-, sub-). Suffixes go at the end (-ly, -ful, -ness). The root is the main part!

📏 Time Challenges 10 min

Measurement — Elapsed Time

You're the Time Master! ⏰ Figure out what time it will be after time passes, or how much time went by. Use the clocks and number lines to help!

✅ Example

Problem: If we leave at 2:45 and it takes 1 hour 20 minutes, when do we arrive?

Think: 2:45 + 1 hour = 3:45. Then + 20 min = 4:05 ✓

💡 Strategy: Add the hours first, then add the minutes. If minutes go past 60, that's another hour!

🔠 Capitalization Dictation 5 min

Shared • Grammar — Capitalization Rules

A parent will read this passage aloud. You write it down with the correct capitalization — then compare to the original! 🔍

👨‍👩‍👦 FOR THE PARENT — Read this aloud slowly:

On Saturday, my friend Carlos and I went to Rocky Mountain National Park. We saw Longs Peak covered in snow. Carlos said, "I want to climb that someday!" We ate lunch by the Big Thompson River and drove home on Highway 34.

💡 Tricky Spots: Names of people, places, days, the word "I", first word of a sentence, and first word someone says in quotes all get capital letters!
