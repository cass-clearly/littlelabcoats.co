🛠️ Test and Improve Your Force Design 🛠️

Physical Science — Forces & Motion | Unit 1, Lesson 6 of 6

📚 Grade: 3rd Grade⏱️ Duration: 50–65 minutes⚡ Subject: Physical Science
🧪🔧
Engineers test, learn from evidence, and improve their designs.
📋 NGSS Standard:
3-PS2-1 — Plan and conduct an investigation to provide evidence of the effects of balanced and unbalanced forces on the motion of an object.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will test and improve your Force Design, compare what changed, and explain what the results show.

💡 The Big Idea

Designing is not just building once. Engineers test, gather evidence, and improve their designs so they work better.

🔬 The Science

A good test checks whether a design meets its goal. Students can compare version 1 and version 2 of a magnetic game or tool by asking what changed and whether the new version works better.

Evidence from tests should guide the redesign. This helps students connect scientific investigation to engineering improvement.

Key vocabulary: test, improve, redesign, evidence, criteria, compare

📦 Materials

  • Your magnetic game or tool from Lesson 5
  • Magnets and magnetic pieces
  • Tape, cardboard, string, scissors, extra supplies for redesign
  • Pencil
  • Printed Student Worksheet

🔬 Let's Get Started

Step 1 — Review the Goal (5 min)
Look back at your original design goal. What was your design supposed to do?
Step 2 — Test Version 1 (10 min)
Run your design at least two times and record what happened.
Step 3 — Choose One Improvement (10 min)
Use your evidence to decide on one or two changes that should help the design work better.
Step 4 — Build Version 2 (15 min)
Make the improvement and label your new version.
Step 5 — Compare Versions (10 min)
Test version 2. Did the design improve? Use evidence to explain your answer.

💬 Discussion Questions

🗣️ Question 1: What problem did your first design have?Look for: a specific issue, such as weak magnet pull, pieces falling, or unclear rules.
🗣️ Question 2: What evidence helped you choose your redesign?Look for: observations from testing, not just guesses.
🗣️ Question 3: Did version 2 work better? How do you know?Look for: a comparison between the first and second versions using evidence.

✨ Extensions

  • 📣 Share It: Explain your redesign to a family member or classmate.
  • 🎯 New Goal: Add a harder challenge or new rule to your design.
  • 📝 Engineer Reflection: Write one sentence about how evidence helps engineers improve ideas.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Normalize redesign: Improvement is a success, not a sign the first attempt failed.
  • Push evidence language: Ask what exactly happened during testing that led to the redesign.
  • Assessment check: Students should compare versions and support the comparison with evidence.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

Detach note: This page is for parent/teacher reference. Student wording may vary.

Acceptable Responses

  • Students should identify a clear problem from testing.
  • The improvement should connect to that problem.
  • The comparison should use evidence from version 1 and version 2.
  • Strong responses name what version 1 did, what changed in version 2, and what proof shows whether version 2 was better, worse, or the same.

What Success Looks Like

  • Student uses evidence to justify the redesign.
  • Student can compare two versions clearly.
  • Student sees redesign as part of engineering.

Troubleshooting / Misconceptions

  • If version 2 still does not work well, that is still useful evidence.
  • If students choose random changes, ask them to connect the change to a specific problem from testing.
