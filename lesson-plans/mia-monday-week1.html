🧠 "Why?" Journal 10 min

Reading — Extended Reasoning & Evidence

Think about whatever you've been reading lately. Now dig deeper — ask "Why?" and then prove your answer using the actual text. Real readers don't just read — they think about what they read. 🤔

  1. Think of 2 "why" questions about your book.
  2. Write your best answer for each one.
  3. Include evidence from the text — a quote, a detail, or a page number.
✅ Example — What Good Text Evidence Looks Like

My question: Why did Meg want to find her father so badly?

My answer: Meg felt incomplete without her dad and blamed herself for not being strong enough to help. On page 42, she says, "I can't just sit here and do nothing while he's out there." This shows she felt responsible and couldn't accept waiting around.

👆 Notice: a specific quote + the reader's own thinking about what it means.

✏️ Freewrite 10 min

Written Expression — Fluency

Set a timer for 10 minutes. Pick a prompt (or make up your own!) and just write. The only rules:

  • 🚫 No stopping
  • 🚫 No erasing
  • 🚫 No editing
  • ✅ Just keep your pen moving!

If you get stuck, write "I don't know what to write" until something comes. It always does. 🌊

Prompt Ideas (pick one or use your own!):

  • If you could have any superpower but only for one hour a day, what would it be and how would you use it?
  • Describe the most interesting place you've ever been — make the reader feel like they're there.
  • You wake up and discover you can talk to animals. What's the first conversation you have?
  • Write a letter to your future self, 10 years from now.
  • What's something you're really good at? How did you get good at it?
