✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Choose two different animals from the unit and explain how one structure from each animal helps it survive, behave, grow, or reproduce.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Use the quiz and short response together: the multiple-choice section checks recognition, while the short response checks whether the student can support a claim with evidence.
  • Good evidence sounds specific: look for an actual named structure plus the job it does.
  • Unit emphasis: strong answers connect structure to survival, behavior, growth, or reproduction — not just to a body-part label.

🔑 Parent Answer Key

Detach note: Student wording may vary if the structure-function reasoning is correct.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. Because different structures do different jobs for survival.
  2. A sensory structure helps an animal detect information.
  3. A feeding structure is matched to the kind of food an animal gets.
  4. Movement structures can help an animal escape danger or reach food.
  5. A shell is an example of a protection structure.
  6. Evidence should name a structure and explain its function.
  7. Several examples usually make an argument stronger than one example.
  8. Structure and function are linked because body parts have jobs.

What Success Looks Like

  • Student names two animals and two structures correctly.
  • Student explains how each structure helps with a real job.
  • Student connects the examples back to the larger claim about animal structures.

Sample acceptable short response

“A hawk’s hooked beak helps it tear food, which supports survival. A fish’s fins help it swim to food and away from danger. These examples show that animal structures help them live by doing important jobs.”
