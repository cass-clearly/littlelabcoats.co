✍️ Short Response — Use Evidence

Choose one weather-related hazard from the unit and explain which design solution has merit for reducing its impact. Use evidence about the hazard and explain why your chosen solution is a good fit.

👩‍🏫 Parent/Teacher Notes

  • Use the quiz and short response together: recognition plus explanation gives a stronger picture of understanding.
  • Good evidence sounds specific: look for weather patterns, chart details, hazard impacts, and a clear reason the solution fits the problem.
  • Fast grading tip: scan for weather vs climate language, hazard impacts, and a reason the chosen design has merit.

✂️ PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

Parent note: Student wording may vary if the Earth Science reasoning is correct.

Multiple-Choice Answer Key

  1. Several observations are needed to notice a pattern.
  2. Climate is the usual long-term pattern in a place.
  3. Charts and graphs help organize evidence.
  4. A hazard can harm people or places.
  5. A drainage solution can reduce flood impact.
  6. The best design matches local weather conditions.
  7. Evidence helps justify a claim.
  8. A weather event is different from climate because it is short-term.

Short Response — What Success Looks Like

  • Student names a weather-related hazard clearly.
  • Student chooses a design solution that matches that hazard.
  • Student explains why the solution has merit by connecting the hazard, the impact, and the evidence.

Short Response — Sample Acceptable Answer

“A flood can damage roads and buildings during heavy rain. A drainage ditch has merit because it moves water away from the building and reduces the flood impact.”
