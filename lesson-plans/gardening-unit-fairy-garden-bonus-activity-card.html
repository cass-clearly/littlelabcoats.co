🧚 Fairy Garden Bonus Activity Card

Garden Detectives bonus hands-on build

Big idea: A tiny garden still needs smart planning. Kids design a miniature habitat using real garden thinking: sunlight, drainage, space, and gentle care.
Keep it simple: Use outdoor rocks, twigs, moss, an old bird house, or even jars and shallow containers if you do not have a big area to work with.
Materials

Gather what you have

  • Small container, old bird house, jar, or corner of a garden bed
  • Outside rocks, pebbles, twigs, bark, acorns, moss, or shells
  • Small spoon or scoop
  • Potting soil if using a container
  • Optional tiny plant, succulent, thyme, or groundcover
  • Optional mini sign or fairy door craft
Build steps

Create the tiny garden

  1. Choose the space and decide whether it gets sun or shade.
  2. Add soil if needed, then place rocks, paths, and tiny features.
  3. Add one simple plant or moss if available.
  4. Make a tiny hiding place, porch, or path from found materials.
  5. Name one reason your fairy garden would help a plant stay healthy.
Science tie-in

Garden planning still matters

  • Does water drain out or pool up?
  • Will the plant get enough sun?
  • Is the container too crowded?
  • What living thing could actually thrive here?
Age stretch

Make it deeper for older kids

  • Sketch a top view before building.
  • Label sun, water, path, and plant space.
  • Explain one constraint you had to solve.
  • Compare a jar garden with an outdoor rock garden.
Parent tip: This works as a joyful capstone or rainy-day bonus. The win is not perfection — it is using real garden-planning ideas in a tiny, imaginative space.
