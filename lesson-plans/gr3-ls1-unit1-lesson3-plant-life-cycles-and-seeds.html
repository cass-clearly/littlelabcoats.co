Plant Life Cycles and Seeds

Life Science — Life Cycles | Unit 1, Lesson 3 of 6

📚 Grade: 3rd Grade ⏱️ Duration: 45–60 minutes 🔬 Subject: Life Science — Life Cycles
🌱🌼🫘
Seeds, seedlings, flowers, and new seeds show a plant life cycle in motion.
📋 NGSS Standard:
3-LS1-1 — Develop models to describe that organisms have unique and diverse life cycles but all have in common birth, growth, reproduction, and death.

🎯 Learning Objective

Students will model a plant life cycle and explain where birth, growth, reproduction, and death appear in that model.

💡 The Big Idea

Plants may look different from animals, but they still follow the same big pattern of birth, growth, reproduction, and death. A seed begins life, a seedling grows, a mature plant reproduces by making new seeds, and the cycle can begin again.

🔬 The Science

A plant life cycle often starts with a seed. When conditions are right, the seed begins to grow into a seedling. The plant gets larger, makes roots, stems, and leaves, and later produces flowers, fruit, cones, or seed pods. Those structures help the plant make new seeds. New seeds begin the pattern again.

🫘
Seed
A new plant begins protected inside a seed.
🌱
Seedling
The young plant starts growing roots, stems, and leaves.
🌿
Mature Plant
The plant grows larger and can make flowers or cones.
🌼
New Seeds
Reproduction makes seeds that can begin the pattern again.

Different plants may have different-looking stages, but the shared pattern still matters most. Scientists care about the model of what happens over time.

Let's Get Started

Step 1 — Reconnect to the unit pattern (5 min)
Review birth, growth, reproduction, and death from Lessons 1 and 2.
Step 2 — Observe a seed and make a claim (8 min)
Ask why a tiny seed counts as the beginning of a plant life cycle.
Step 3 — Build the plant model (12 min)
Walk through seed → seedling → mature plant → flower or seed pod → new seeds.
Step 4 — Compare plants to animals (10 min)
Compare a bean plant to one familiar animal from earlier lessons.
Step 5 — Explain with evidence (10 min)
Use the worksheet to explain how the plant model proves plants have life cycles too.

Discussion Questions

Why does a seed count as the beginning of a plant life cycle?Look for answers like: the new plant begins inside the seed, so the seed is part of birth and the start of growth.
How is a plant life cycle the same as an animal life cycle?They might say both begin life, grow, reproduce, and die, even though the stages look different.
Where do you see reproduction in a plant life cycle?Look for flowers, cones, fruit, or seed pods making new seeds that can become new plants.
Why do scientists use models for plant life cycles?They might say models help us see the order of stages and explain how one stage leads to the next.
What would happen if a plant never made seeds?Students may say the cycle could not begin again for a new plant of the same kind.

Extensions

Try one more plant: Add a second plant, such as a sunflower, pumpkin, or pine tree, and compare how its life cycle stages look similar and different.

Family challenge: Plant a bean seed and keep a short observation journal.

Parent/Teacher Notes

Teaching move: Keep returning to the idea that making seeds is how many plants reproduce.

Important misconception to catch: Some students think seeds are just “plant crumbs.” Emphasize that a seed is the beginning of a new organism.

🔑 PARENT ANSWER KEY — Detach before giving worksheet to students

1️⃣ Put the Plant Life Cycle in Order — Sample Acceptable Answers

Accept seed → seedling/sprout → young or mature plant → flower/pod/cone → new seeds.

2️⃣ Show the Big Pattern in a Plant — Sample Ideas

  • Birth: the plant begins as a seed
  • Growth: the seedling and mature plant get bigger
  • Reproduction: the plant makes flowers, fruit, cones, or seed pods that produce new seeds
  • Death: the plant eventually dies after its life

3️⃣ Use Your Model as Evidence — Model Response

“My model shows that a bean plant begins as a seed, grows into a larger plant, makes new seeds, and dies. That is evidence that plants have life cycles too.”

4️⃣ Compare a Plant and an Animal — Sample Ideas

Same: both begin life, grow, reproduce, and die.
Different: a plant begins as a seed and reproduces by making new seeds, while many animals begin in eggs or are born live.

✅ What Success Looks Like

  • Student can place the plant stages in a logical order.
  • Student can identify where reproduction happens in a plant model.
  • Student can use the model as evidence instead of only naming parts.
  • Student can connect a plant example back to the same big pattern used earlier in the unit.

🛠️ Troubleshooting / Teaching Notes

  • If a student thinks the flower is the end of the cycle, ask what happens after the flower or pod forms.
  • If a student skips death, remind them that all living things die even if the next generation begins.
  • If a student gives only a surface comparison, prompt: “Where do you see birth, growth, reproduction, and death in both models?”
