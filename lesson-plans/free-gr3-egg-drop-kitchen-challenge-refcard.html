Egg Drop Kitchen Challenge Reference Card

Grade 3
Engineers reduce the force of impact by spreading it out, slowing it down, and redesigning after each test.
Key vocabulary
impactforcecushionredesign
What to notice
  • A successful design manages what happens when the egg lands.
  • Soft or crumpling materials can absorb some force.
  • Success criterion: the egg stays uncracked, or cracks less than in the first version.
  • Redesign is part of engineering, not a sign of failure.
Materials snapshot
  • Raw eggs
  • Paper, tape, cups, straws, or padding
  • Drop zone
  • Notebook
Talk-about prompts
  • How did your design reduce the force on the egg?
  • What happened at impact?
  • Which version protected the egg better?
  • Which evidence helped you improve?
Why it matters

Engineering gets stronger when students test, notice what failed, and redesign with evidence. This challenge helps Grade 3 learners connect force, impact, and improvement to a result they can actually measure.

Try this next: Try a low drop, a higher drop, and a redesign round to compare how engineering changes the outcome. Record exactly which version protected the egg best.
