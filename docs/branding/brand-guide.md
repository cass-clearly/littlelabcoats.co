## Which version to use

### Use the transparent version when
- placing the logo in the site header/navigation
- placing the logo in hero sections
- placing the logo over colored, gradient, dark, or photographic backgrounds
- generating favicon and touch-icon derivatives
- any situation where the logo should float cleanly without a visible box

Preferred file:
- `images/site/llc-logo-serena-approved-2026-04-10.png`

### Use the non-transparent version when
- exporting a flat mockup that specifically needs a baked-in background
- preparing a presentation/doc where transparency causes rendering issues
- sharing a static proof that needs the exact approved filled treatment

Preferred file:
- `branding/selected/2026-04-10-serena-logo-base/little-lab-coats-selected-logo-base-openai-edit.png`

## Site usage rules

### Header / nav
- Prefer the full approved logo mark, not the older badge crop.
- Target rendered height: **64–72px** on desktop.
- On small screens, scale down proportionally rather than cropping.
- Keep enough clear space that no text, buttons, or borders crowd the mark.

### Footer
- Use the same approved logo family for consistency.
- Target rendered height: **32–40px**.
- On dark footers, use the transparent logo asset rather than old cream-background versions.

### Hero / homepage brand moment
- When a large branded hero image is needed, use the approved transparent logo.
- Target rendered width/height should preserve aspect ratio and avoid stretching.
- Do not swap in the older circular badge unless a human explicitly asks for a badge-only treatment.

## Clear-space guidance

Use at least:
- **0.5x logo height** of space above and below
- **0.35x logo height** minimum on left and right

Practical rule for current site layouts:
- if the logo is rendered at 72px tall, leave roughly **24–36px** breathing room around it whenever possible.

## Do not do this

- Do not use `images/site/llc-badge-transparent.png` as the default site logo anymore.
- Do not use `images/site/llc-badge-cream.jpg` on canonical public-facing surfaces.
- Do not mix old badge art in nav with new logo art in hero/footer.
- Do not stretch, squash, crop, recolor, or add shadows/glows to the approved mark.
- Do not place the transparent mark on a busy background without enough contrast.
