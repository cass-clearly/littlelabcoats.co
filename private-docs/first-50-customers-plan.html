Little Lab Coats Growth Plan

How to Get to Your First 50 Customers

You do not need viral reach first. You need a clean path from social attention → freebie → conversation → purchase, repeated consistently with one or two hero offers.

Daily posting Free lesson lead magnet Warm DM follow-up 1–2 hero bundles Track what converts
The goal is not “be everywhere.” The goal is: make it very easy for the right homeschool parent to say yes.

1) What to Focus On First

One clear freebie

Lead with one irresistible free lesson or mini bundle that solves a real parent problem fast.

  • Easy win
  • Feels useful immediately
  • Naturally leads into a paid unit

One or two hero offers

Don’t spread your energy across every unit at once. Push the bundles most likely to convert first.

  • Strongest topic appeal
  • Cleanest preview
  • Easiest “yes” price point

Personal follow-up

Your early sales edge is warmth and specificity. People buy faster when they feel seen.

  • Ask grade
  • Ask science interest
  • Recommend the right bundle

2) The Funnel You Want

1
Post

Reel, post, carousel, comment, story

2
Freebie CTA

“Comment WEATHER” or “DM me for the free lesson”

3
Conversation

Ask one or two helpful questions

4
Offer

Recommend the matching paid bundle

5
Close + Follow Up

Checkout link, delivery, testimonial ask

If a post gets attention but does not lead to a DM, email, or checkout, it is not yet doing enough business work.

3) Weekly Numbers That Get You There

20–30
new freebie leads per week
Comments, DMs, or email captures
10–15
meaningful parent conversations
Short, personal, warm follow-up
2–5
bundle purchases per week
This is enough to reach 50 faster than it feels

If you convert even a small slice of warm freebie leads each week, 50 customers becomes a process problem, not a miracle problem.

4) Daily Rhythm

Daily

  • Post once on Instagram and/or Facebook
  • Use one clear CTA tied to the freebie or bundle
  • Reply to every relevant comment and DM
  • Start 3–5 warm conversations with interested parents
  • Track who asked for what

Weekly

  • Review which post brought the most leads
  • Review which freebie led to the most bundle conversations
  • Review which bundle actually sold
  • Double down on the format/topic that moved people
  • Ask at least 2 happy buyers for a testimonial

5) What to Say in DMs

When someone wants the free lesson

Script:

“Absolutely — happy to send it. What grade are your kids in, and do they usually get most excited about animals, weather, space, or hands-on building?”

This keeps it personal and gives you the setup for a later recommendation.

When they engage with the freebie

Script:

“If your kids liked that one, I think you’d really love the full unit — it keeps the same style but gives you the complete sequence, printables, and extras. Want me to send the link?”

Soft close

Script:

“This is the one I’d point you to based on what you said: [bundle]. It’s especially good for [specific reason]. Here’s the link if you want it.”

After purchase

Script:

“Thank you so much. I’d love to hear how your kids like it once you’ve tried the first lesson — even one sentence helps me a lot.”

6) What to Track

Lead tracking

  • Name
  • Platform: Instagram or Facebook
  • How they came in: post, reel, story, comment, DM, share
  • Freebie requested
  • Grade / interests
  • Offered product
  • Bought or not yet

Questions to ask weekly

  • Which post brought the most real conversations?
  • Which freebie created the most buying intent?
  • Which parents sounded excited but stalled?
  • What objections kept showing up?
  • Which bundle is acting like a true hero product?

7) Best Bet for Your First 50

  1. Lead with one excellent free lesson parents actually want.
  2. Use that free lesson to start warm conversations.
  3. Personally recommend the right paid bundle.
  4. Keep checkout and fulfillment friction-free.
  5. Collect testimonials early and reuse them in posts.
  6. Repeat the same funnel until it becomes boring and reliable.

8) What Not to Do

Don’t chase every product at once

Too many offers makes early buyers hesitate.

Don’t drop links cold too fast

Conversation converts better than link-dumping at this stage.

Don’t mistake activity for traction

Likes are nice. Conversations and purchases matter more.

9) Your First 50 Customer Operating Rule

Every day, create attention. Every day, turn some of that attention into actual conversations. Every week, turn those conversations into purchases and proof.
