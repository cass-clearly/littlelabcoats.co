## “Wow Summer Science Series” — outline only

The goal here is **quick setup, strong visual payoff, not cliché, and very filmable for social**.

### 1. Vanishing Arrows Lab
**Big wow:** the arrow flips direction through a glass of water
- Science idea: light refraction
- Why it works: instant, surprising, zero kid convincing required
- Supplies: paper, marker, glass/jar, water
- Reel value: perfect for a fast “wait WHAT?” hook
- Lesson angle: how light bends and changes what our eyes see

### 2. Dancing Raisin Fizz Lab
**Big wow:** raisins rise and fall on their own in fizzy liquid
- Science idea: gas bubbles and buoyancy
- Why it works: weird movement = instant kid delight
- Supplies: clear glass, raisins, unflavored sparkling water or soda water
- Reel value: motion-heavy and easy to capture close-up
- Lesson angle: bubbles attach, lift, pop, and release

### 3. Leakproof Bag Stab Test
**Big wow:** pencils go through a water-filled zip bag without it spilling everywhere
- Science idea: polymer chains in plastic
- Why it works: high suspense, very strong watch-time moment
- Supplies: zip-top bag, water, sharp pencils, tray
- Reel value: “this looks fake” energy
- Lesson angle: some materials can stretch around objects and still hold together

### 4. Color Potion pH Lab
**Big wow:** cabbage indicator changes color with different kitchen liquids
- Science idea: acids, bases, and indicators
- Why it works: colorful, magical-looking, but still real science
- Supplies: red cabbage indicator, small cups, lemon juice, baking soda water, soap water, etc.
- Reel value: gorgeous visual transformations
- Lesson angle: invisible chemical differences can create visible color changes
