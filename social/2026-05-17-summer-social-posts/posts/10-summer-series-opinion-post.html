Would Anyone Want a Short Summer STEM Series?

I’m thinking about a special 4-week / 4-lesson summer unit for families who want a little structure without turning summer into school.

The idea:

A light, fun summer mini-unit with four engaging lessons your kids can look forward to each week. Something simple enough to use, but special enough to feel worth grabbing for the season.

What I’m wondering

  • Would this actually be helpful for your family?
  • What topics would you want most?
  • Would $20 feel reasonable for a summer mini-unit?

Possible themes

  • kitchen chemistry
  • nature explorers
  • summer magic science
  • engineering challenges

Materials question

Would you rather I build this around common household materials you probably already have, or make it feel more special with a short supply list to gather first?

Best-case version

Something that keeps kids busy, gives your week a little rhythm, and does not make you feel like you signed up for a second full curriculum over the summer.

Post prompt

Would you want a short summer STEM series for your kids? If yes, I’d love to know:

What topic would your kids be most excited about?
Household materials only, or would you enjoy a slightly more special supply list?
Would a $20 mini-unit feel like an easy yes for your family?
