5 STEM Activities for Kindergarten

Five easy, hands-on science invitations with enough direction to actually use.

How to use this: Pick one activity, gather simple supplies, and keep it short. The goal is not a perfect lesson — it is noticing, testing, comparing, and talking together.

1. Ramp Roll Rescue

Need: books, cardboard, toy car

Try it: Build one low ramp and one steep ramp. Roll the same car down both.

  • Which ramp is faster?
  • Which ramp lets the car go farther?

2. Sink or Float Bowl

Need: bowl of water, 6 household objects

Try it: Predict first, then test each item in water.

  • Which items surprised your child?
  • Did any heavy item float?

3. Magnet Hunt

Need: magnet, safe items from around the house

Try it: Make a “sticks” pile and a “does not stick” pile.

  • What do the sticking items have in common?
  • Which guesses were wrong?

4. Leaf Detective Walk

Need: 3 leaves, outdoor space

Try it: Compare leaves by size, color, shape, and texture.

  • Which leaf is smoothest?
  • Which one is biggest?

5. Paper Bridge Rescue

Need: paper, 2 cups, pennies or small books

Try it: Test a flat sheet first. Then fold or roll it and test again.

  • Which design holds more weight?
  • How did changing the shape help?
