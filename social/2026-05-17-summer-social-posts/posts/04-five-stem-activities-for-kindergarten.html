5 STEM Activities for Kindergarten

More play, less worksheet energy — five easy ideas that keep little ones busy, curious, and learning.

Why this version works

These feel like fun parent activities you can do today — not mini lesson plans wearing a social-post costume.

  • simple supplies
  • quick setup
  • actually fun for little kids
  • learning still sneaks in
1

Pom-Pom Tube Drop

toilet paper tubes + tape + pom-poms10 min
Do

Tape cardboard tubes to a wall or big box and drop pom-poms through to build a little maze.

Watch for
  • fast vs. slow paths
  • which turns work best
2

Sink-or-Float Treasure Tub

water bowl + small objects10 min
Do

Guess first, then test spoons, corks, blocks, leaves, or coins.

Watch for
  • surprises
  • great prediction talk
3

Magnet Treasure Hunt

magnet + safe house items10 min
Do

Search for things that stick and sort them into yes and no piles.

Watch for
  • pattern spotting
  • surprising “no” items
4

Leaf Detective Walk

3 leaves + magnifying glassoutdoor reset
Do

Collect a few leaves and compare size, edges, color, and texture.

Watch for
  • same vs. different
  • nature words
5

Balloon Rocket Race

string + straw + tape + balloonbig kid wow
Do

Thread string through a straw, tape a balloon to it, blow it up, and let it zoom across the room.

Watch for
  • which balloon goes farther
  • how air makes it move
